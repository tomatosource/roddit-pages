Some interesting things I noticed while combing through these games:

  • David Robinson dropped 71 while missing 7 free throws; similarly Donovan Mitchell dropped 71 while missing 5 free throws

  • Pistol Pete’s potential 71 was with no three point line. He had many deep shots in this game, so he could have been pushing 80 had there been a 3-point line then

  • Baylor from 1959 is the oldest game on this list, while Luka from this year is the most recent

  • Wilt’s games spanned by far the longest period of time (from 1961 to 1969). Baylor is the only other guy with multiple years on the list (1959 and 1960)

  • Wilt had three opportunities to set the Single Game Playoff scoring record. He scored 56 (missing 10 free throws), 53 (missing 11 free throws), and 46 (missing 17! free throws)

  • Completely irrelevant, but there were multiple players that scored 59 points while missing just one free throw. From what I could find: James Harden (17 of 18), Devin Booker (16 of 17), Anthony Davis (9 of 10), Purvis Short (15 of 16), and Wilt (11 of 12), all missed out on 60 point nights because of a single free throw. Crazy!
