How well do you all think Zach LaVine, Coby White, and Josh Giddey will mesh on the floor together for the Chicago Bulls?

As we all know, the Bulls made an attempt to trade Zach LaVine during the offseason but found no takers. They were resigned fairly early into the offseason that LaVine would not be moved and preparing themselves to carry him on the roster going into the season ahead.

Now news has come out of an overall attempt at mending fences and healing tensions throughout the summer between the Bulls FO, Billy Donovan, and Zach LaVine. Karnisovas came forward and said the front office didn’t have appetite for a full scale teardown and wouldn’t trade LaVine for pennies on the dollar. So here we are, with training camps set to open in a few weeks, with LaVine still on the Bulls roster.

The Bulls moved on from DeRozan and traded Caruso for Giddey. Coby White had a breakout campaign last season in a featured offensive role and is someone who can score without heavy usage, but how he adjusts to playing with LaVine who may be playing to prove himself and will probably look at this team as His, will be interesting to watch. Giddey is a talented playmaker and had a good run for Team Australia in Olympic comp but he has his notable warts. LaVine can pass, but he’s not quite the playmaker DeMar has become historically. He’s not a natural decision maker, or at least hasn’t shown that he is up to this point, specifically when he’s been the #1 option in Chicago.
