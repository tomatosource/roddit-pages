NBA Superstars health over time graph (2003-2024)

In light of recent news, I was wondering to myself how much of an outlier Kawhi Leonard’s health was in relation to his superstar peers. I grabbed some quick data and voila, here’s 13 NBA superstars of the Lebron era and the trends for what percentage of regular season games they played in over time.

Behold!!

The above graph is the CUMULATIVE percentage of available games played by each player. I tried to color appropriately but distinctively.

Durability rankings of the guys I grabbed:

  1. Nikola Jokic (93.6%) - Of course, he’s only 29 so he hasn’t hit the old man injury phase yet
  2. Russell Westbrook (90.9%) - Maybe the true iron man of the list; Missed 0 games for first 5 seasons
  3. James Harden (89.6%) - Consistently durable, with slightly less miles on him due to early sixth man role
  4. Giannis Antetokounmpo (89.5%) - Started strong with a healthy rookie contract and has been fairly average in health since then. Still relatively young for this list
  5. Lebron James (88.3%) - Known early on for being an iron man, but slow decline has tracked the average slope of leaguewide health vs. age pretty dead on
  6. Damian Lillard (87.1%) - Very healthy in his 20s; steeper decline than most in his 30s
  7. Luka Doncic (84.2%) - mildly, repeat, mildly concerned that he couldn’t rank higher at age 25
  8. Chris Paul (83.4%) - memetically always injured in his prime; has generally kept the slope straighter than most in his 30s but it was a low baseline to maintain
  9. Carmelo Anthony (82.6%) - I tried to avoid anyone who dropped off so much that he actually wasn’t on a team for some of the eligible seasons, but he was important enough to include. Feel free to mentally bump him up a percent or two for pure health, but his late 20s were not kind to him either - he actually tracks Paul’s graph closely
  10. Dwyane Wade (80.6%) - by far the roughest early-career health of any superstar, even with Kawhi on this chart
  11. Stephen Curry (79.9%) - this actually reaches the level of pretty big all-time what-if scenario; impressive 5-year run in his 20s to briefly break from last place to mid-pack, though
  12. Kevin Durant (78.0%) - catastrophic 30s for someone looking to push his way into the all-time top 10 discussion
  13. Kawhi Leonard (67.4%) - don’t want to dunk on the guy but this is such a wild outlier; Kevin Durant (12) is closer to Lebron (5) than Kawhi.

I wanted to do even more players but the graph was already cramped enough.
