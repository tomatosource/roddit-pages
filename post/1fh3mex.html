Pure scorers vs dominant scorers: which is more valuable?

We’ve heard all the weird little anecdotes used to describe different ways to score the ball. Hoopers, ethical scoring, run and dunk, etc. From what I’ve seen the elite scorers of the NBA across its existence generally fall into two different categories:

The “pure” scorers: Generally smaller and/or more agile players that rely on skill and finesse to generate buckets. They tend to take shots further away from the basket which can be less efficient, but stresses defenses out a little more, and they use their mobility and footwork to generate offense moreso than others. Examples are Michael Jordan, Kobe Bryant, James Harden, Allen Iverson, Kyrie Irving, Kevin Durant.

The “dominant” scorers: Generally bigger, stronger players that rely on their size and physical abilities to generate buckets. They tend to live in the paint and dominate smaller players, which leads to overall better efficiency since they take more shots at the rim, but perhaps have less range and skill than their smaller contemporaries. Examples are LeBron James, Giannis Antetokounmpo, Shaquille O’Neal, Wilt Chamberlain, Russell Westbrook, Karl Malone.

Now obviously a lot of these players don’t ONLY rely on skill or ONLY rely on their physical gifts to get buckets, but there seems to be 2 distinct approaches to scoring the basketball, and I’m curious about what others think if one method is more valuable than another.
