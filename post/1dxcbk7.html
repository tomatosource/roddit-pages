The Atlanta Hawks have $23 million trade exception. It is set to expire in 24 hours.

As a result of the John Collins salary dump (TL;DR on the initial deal is the Hawks got Rudy Gay and a 2nd for their former #2 option to Trae Young), the Atlanta Hawks opened up a trade exception that was initially reported to be \(25.3 million but was finalized as a \)23 million trade exception. It is one of the largest exceptions opened up in NBA history, with the current record being a $28.5 million exception the Boston Celtics opened up via Gordon Hayward’s sign and trade to the Hornets back in November 2020.

That exception was ultimately wasted, as the Celtics failed to utilize it to bring in anyone new prior to its expiration - in fact they wound up losing some of their own pending free agents in 2021. Now, the Atlanta Hawks are in possession of a similarly large trade exception and assets to potentially bring in a player of note to shore up their depth - or if they are planning to move forward with a rebuild, a player on a contract of notable size to acquire more assets via draft picks - however said exception is soon to expire.

On July 8th, 2024, the Atlanta Hawks will no longer possess this notably large trade exception. If it expires, this would mean the Hawks essentially traded away John Collins for…a protected second round pick which may not even convey; Rudy Gay was eventually traded (Along with a second rounder from the Hawks, TyTy Washington and Usman Garuba) for Patty Mills who was eventually waived by Atlanta.

Ironically, both the aforementioned Celtics exception and this current one involved Danny Ainge; the current GM of the Utah Jazz was in charge of the Boston Celtics at the time.

As for the player, John Collins is rumored to be (back) on the trade block after the Utah Jazz were “underwhelmed” by his 2023⁄24 season where his stats more closely resembled his prior seasons with Atlanta.
