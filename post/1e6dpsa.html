55th rarely find success - only 2 of last 13 picks lasted in the NBA [OC]

In a thread discussing Bronny so far lack of success, I decided to look up how many of the last few 55th picks lasted. What you find is that a significant portion of the last 8 picks in the draft never even play an NBA game. But if we specifically look at the 55th pick, you need to go back to 2011 before you find someone who had a substantial career in the NBA (E’Twaun Moore played 10 years in the league).

2011 - E’Twaun Moore played 10 years in the league
2012 - played 7 games in the NBA
2013 - played 4 years (208 games) in the NBA
2014 - played 1 year, 64 games in the NBA
2015 - Never player in NBA
2016 - played 5 games in the NBA
2017 - played 1 year (10 games) in the NBA
2018 - played 2 games in the NBA
2019 - played 3 years (53 games) in the NBA
2020 - played 2 years, 33 games, out of NBA
2021 - In the NBA (198 games so far) – Aaron Wiggins with OKC currently
2022 - played 1 year, 23 games in the NBA
2023 - played 1 games in the NBA (so far), mostly in G-league.

The reality is that most 55th picks are almost never NBA type talents. Bronny may or may have a lasting NBA career. But if he doesn’t, it would be the norm, not the exception. Most importantly it’s completely normal for them to struggle.
