In the 1967-68 season, Lenny Wilkens, a 9-time All-Star, was voted 2nd in the MVP race, but he was not selected for the All-NBA Team

In 1967-68, Lenny Wilkens finished second in the MVP race, just behind Wilt Chamberlain. Despite this, he wasn’t selected for the All-NBA First or Second Teams. Instead, Oscar Robertson, Dave Bing, Jerry West, and Hal Greer were chosen for the guard spots.

He is also the player with the most All-Star appearances who never got an All-NBA Team selection.
