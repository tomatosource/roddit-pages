If NBA players were classical composers

  • George Mikan = Claudio Monteverdi. Milkman (Monteverdi composed in the Renaissance era).
  • Bill Russell = Johann Sebastian Bach. The greatest of his era and the absolute master of a certain aspect of the game (Bach defines the Baroque period and was as good as counterpoint as Bill was at defense).
  • Wilt Chamberlain = Antonio Vivaldi. Known best for one high-scoring game (Vivaldi’s most famous work by far is the Four Seasons) but still put up crazy volume stats (Vivaldi wrote hundreds of concertos for various instruments).
  • Kareem Abdul-Jabbar = Joseph Haydn. Known for his dominance with a particular type of shot (Haydn invented the symphony as we know it) and soared up the scoring leaderboards with it (Haydn wrote 104 of them, the most of any major composer).
  • Larry Bird = Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart. Dominant all-time great who had to retire at 35 due to injuries (Mozart died at 35).
  • Magic Johnson = Franz Schubert. Also retired too early, but due to an STD (Schubert died from syphilis at 31).
  • Michael Jordan = Ludwig van Beethoven. The GOAT.
  • Kobe Bryant = Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky. Known for his elite footwork (Tchaikovsky wrote many famous ballets).
  • Tim Duncan = Johannes Brahms. The Big Fundamental (Brahms’ style was less flashy than many of his contemporaries, but he still dominated at fundamental composition forms such as symphonies, concertos, and chamber music).
  • Manu Ginobili = Richard Wagner. Known for his skillful integration of theatrics into the game (Wagner was renowned for his operas) and having four rings (Wagner’s best-known work, the Ring Cycle, is a series of four operas).
  • Shaquille O’Neal = Sergei Rachmaninoff. Known best for his unmatched physical dominance (Rachmaninoff was a virtuoso pianist and had very large hands) and also switched conferences in his prime (Rachmaninoff moved from Russia to America mid-career).
  • LeBron James = Igor Stravinsky. All-time great with longevity and multiple primes (Stravinsky lived until 90 and had multiple stylistic periods) with a defining career moment that angered fans (his ballet Rite of Spring incited riots when it premiered in Paris).
  • Stephen Curry = Arnold Schoenberg. Revolutionized the way the game is played (Schoenberg and the Second Viennese School pioneered atonal music).
  • Yao Ming = George Gershwin. Helped popularize the game on a new continent (Gershwin was among the first great American composers).
  • Kyrie Irving = Dmitri Shostakovich. Had career problems due to government policies (Shostakovich was condemned by the USSR).
