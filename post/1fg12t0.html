Looking at the 5 Year Peak D-RAPM of defenders(Off-ball vs On-ball)

5 year Peak RAPM - RAPM is essentially a stat that is meant to adjust for the weaknesses of plus-minus. It adjusts for opponent quality, and team quality.

Considered Elite On-Ball Defenders but Weaker Off-Ball

Lu dort: 1.1(Rank 597)

Klay Thompson: 1.1(Rank 597)

Kobe Bryant: 0.2(Rank 1310)

Jaylen Brown: 1.3(Rank 491)

Avery Bradley: 2.0(Rank 262)

The guys with the highest D-RAPM peaks ever are typically rim protectors or are considered some of the best help defenders ever. Draymond Green(5.4), Kevin Garnett(7.1), Tim Duncan(5.8), Giannis(5.0), Embiid(5.0) are all around the top 10 ever in D-RAPM peaks.

Players like Robert Covington who is a specialist in help defense has a D-RAPM of 3.9 and has the 38th best D-RAPM peak ever. This isn’t to say that D-RAPM is a perfect stat for evaluating specific defenders or comparing specific defenders.

But I think the pattern holds, where weak off-ball defenders don’t look great most of the time. Weak on-ball defenders but good off-ball defenders who might be viewed as negative defenders, can actually look quite similar in impact relative to elite defenders who are considered elite because of their on-ball defense.
