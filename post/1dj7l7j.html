The Boston Celtics led the regular season in 3PA and 3PM and won the title, the first team since (and the only team besides) the 1994 and 1995 Houston Rockets to do so

We’ve heard about the “three-point revolution” and the explosion in analytics, but interestingly, if you look at the list of 3-point statistic leaders, the Celtics are the first team to lead the league in 3PA and 3PM and win the title since 1995. No other team besides them and the 94+95 Rockets have led in either and won the title.

There have been some teams that led the league in three-point percentage and still won the title - the 1986 Celtics (5th in 3PA), the 1987 Lakers (5th in 3PA), the 2014 Spurs (17th in 3PA), the 2015 Warriors (4th in 3PA), and the 2018 Warriors (16th in 3PA). But none of them were even close to leading the league in 3PA or 3PM. It’s intriguing that the Celtics are the first team in the analytics era to take the “go full steam with threes” approach and still win the title.

The Golden State Warriors also never led the league in 3PA or 3PM in any of their title runs: the Houston Rockets did so in 2015, 2017, and 2018, and the Minnesota Timberwolves did so in 2022. Funnily, the one year where the Golden State Warriors did lead the league in 3PA, 3PM, and 3P% was 2016.
