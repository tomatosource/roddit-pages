I wish we put more attention towards regular season historic feats.

In the MLB, almost every super rare feat in the regular season is celebrated and talked about for years to come. Examples:

  • 2021: Ohtani’s first full year pitching and hitting
  • 2022: Judge breaking the HR record for non-steroids and also AL record
  • 2023: Acuna having 40 homers and 70+ steals

Yet in the NBA, James Harden’s 36 PPG season isn’t talked about enough just because he didn’t advance far in the playoffs. Or Steph Curry’s 2016 historic 3 point efficiency season on mass volume. Or the few 50/40/90 seasons. Why does this all get overshadowed by playoff stats? Why don’t NBA fans talk about it more often like how baseball fans talk about regular season stats? How come this only matters what happens in April-June and not October-March?

You don’t see MLB fans talk about Judge’s 2022 playoff performance. He went liquid nitrogen yet everyone praises his ability to hit 62 homers. Or Acuna who was essentially a ghost before Halloween.
