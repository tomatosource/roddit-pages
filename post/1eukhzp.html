Standing reach is the most important physical stat in basketball, but no one talks about it.

Intro photo.

We always focus on height when we talk about basketball players and what positions they can play, but height doesn’t take into account wingspan or shoulder height (look at this comparison between Bynum and Gasol’s shoulders, it might be a reason why Bynum blocked 2.5 shots per-36 that year and Gasol despite much greater experience blocked 1.5).

Standing reach, on the other hand, takes into account everything. Height, wingspan, and shoulder height into a single stat. It helps show why Elton Brand, despite being listed at only 6’8” when he entered the NBA, was able to play both power forward and center, averaged 20 and 10 consistently before his injuries and block 2 shots a game (his standing reach was 9’2”), and Marvin Bagley despite being listed at 6’10” seems to struggle in the frontcourt (standing reach only 8’9”).

In this pic, you can see pretty clearly how much of a different standing reach really makes. Boban is 4-5 inches taller than Davis, but actually has a 7” higher standing reach (9’0” vs 9’7”). Which is illustrated there, Davis can’t really contest what Boban is doing without jumping, and Boban could shoot by just waiting for Davis to come down from a jump.

This might also help show why Victor is so insane as a shotblocker. Yao Ming, listed at 7’6” had a 9’8” standing reach, but Victor apparently has a 10’ standing reach, a full 4-inches higher.

The other important stat that relates to this, and also takes athleticism into account, is max vertical reach. Which can encompass all of it. IIRC the record of around 12’6” belongs to Shaq and Dwight Howard. Shaq was a great athlete at 7’1”, and Howard was of course a unique combination of super-high shoulders and insane athleticism despite being only 6’10.5”.

I think most NBA centers need to have at least a 9’0” standing reach, but really I think the standard is more like 9’2”. So that’s really what to look for when you scout a big man, height almost doesn’t matter at all IMO.
