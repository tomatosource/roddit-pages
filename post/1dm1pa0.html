10 years ago, the San Antonio Spurs won the NBA title without a single Spur averaging more than 18 points per game

It was a beautiful display of team basketball. Not a single player also had to average more than 35.2 minutes per game, which was averaged by Boris Diaw and Tony Parker.

It was also one of the more efficient scoring Finals for the winning team. The Spurs shot 53% from the field, 47% from 3 and 75% from the foul line. They only lost game 2 by 2 points and won their 4 games by an average point differential of 18.

This was also the last time a team won the Finals without a single player on the winning team averaging less than 20 points.
