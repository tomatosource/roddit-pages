[OC] Unmasking MVP Controversies: The Accuracy of WS/48 in Spotlighting Controversial Selections Since 1960.

Every year, the choice of MVP often leads to disappointment for some fanbases. Some years more often than others.

That’s where win shares per 48 minutes (WS/48) comes in. WS/48 evens the playing field by evaluating each player’s impact as if they all played the same number of minutes. Unlike regular win shares, which are cumulative, WS/48 measures how efficiently a player contributes. While a fairly flawed advanced statistic due to the box score nature of the statistic (over smaller samples), it does a fairly decent job of parsing through the cream of the crop.

I decided to look through all MVP seasons since 1960 and pull out all top 3 finishes. Among the top three candidates if the 2nd or 3rd placed candidate had a higher WS/48, they were selected as potentially contentious selections.

How does this stack up to the eye-test or general consensus among fans?
