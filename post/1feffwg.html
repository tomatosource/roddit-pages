What made Muggsy Bogues so special?

Lately I’ve been thinking about Muggsy Bogues and how he basically did the unthinkable for a guy his size: he not only played in the NBA but also got drafted in the lottery and played 14 seasons. So with that in mind I have so many questions.

What made him so special and allowed him to have the career he had? I know his calling card was defense and he was a pretty good passer but so were a lot of other point guards at the time and they were average height for the NBA. Was that really all there was to his game? There hasn’t been a player at his height before he came into the league and there hasn’t been since. So what did scouts see in him even at the college level that made them say “You know what this guy belongs in the NBA, I’d like to take him with my lottery pick”? He was also a standout college player but so are so many other D-I players and even they didn’t sniff the league let alone play there for 14 years. What other tools allowed him to get playing time on the court apart from his very low center of gravity and his nose for the ball? And (this might be more of a speculative question why didn’t he leave much of a legacy for smaller guards when he left? Why didn’t NBA scouts look at smaller players differently and decide they could be an NBA point guard even when they already had a precedent for a smaller player playing and lasting long in the league?
