Were the 2008-2010 Cavs supporting cast really THAT bad?

All the time people say “LeBron had one of the worst supporting casts of all time” and his first stint Cavs were absolutely horrible. But how can that be? They had the best record in the league for two straight years if not three (misremembering). Were they really THAT bad?

Okay sure, it is true that LeBron was the only superstar on that team but the Cavs were not unique in that situation then. Remember it was a different time then. Most teams had just one superstar and having more than one was not common then. Dwight Howard was the only superstar on the Magic. Dwyane Wade was the only star on the Heat. You will probably say “but what about the Lakers and the Celtics then?” but they were the exception rather than the rule. Even if you look at the NBA today, if the Sixers for example lose Embiid they are also toast! The whole “this player is carrying the whole team” statement is imo misleading or if you are going to say it then you need to concede that that is true for every team then that has a superstar.

“Oh but if you take LeBron away from that team that team is suddenly the worst team in the NBA.” Sure but that could have been said about most other teams back then with a star! Lose Wade and the heat were also doomed. Same with the Magic if they lost Dwight. The team was built around Lebron imo, rather than “LeBron carried that team on his back.” The Cavs actually did an excellent job imo of surrounding an excellent passer and driver in LeBron with excellent role players who were able to shoot the 3, such as Mo Williams and Delonte West. Yes, it is true that they lacked a capable big man and way past their prime old men Shaq and Big Z were not going to cut it, but I think it’s a fair criticism that LeBron jumped ship before they were able to complete the team and get to the championship. If they had gotten a good center there is no doubt in my mind the Cavs would have won the championship with that team.
