Giannis spearheads Greece’s 18-man roster for the Olympic Qualifying Tournament in Piraeus, awaits matchup with Towns (Dominican Republic) and potential matchup with Luka (Slovenia)

Source

Former two-time NBA MVP Giannis Antetokounmpo is the main star on Greece’s roster for the Olympic Qualifying Tournament on their home soil, which is scheduled for the start of July.

In addition to Giannis, the roster includes players such as: the reigning Euroleague Final Four MVP Kostas Sloukas, Euroleague all-time assists and steals leader Nick Calathes, Euroleague Best Defender Thomas Walkup, Euroleague champion Dinos Mitroglou and former All-Euroleague selection, 7’3” Georgios Papagiannis, among others.

Greece will be playing against the Dominican Republic and Egypt in the group phase of their Olympic Qualifying Tournament, and the top two teams from the group match up in the playoffs (semi-final and final, single-elimination) against the top two teams from the group consisting of Slovenia, Croatia and New Zealand.

The winner of each of the four OQTs will earn a spot at the Paris Olympics.
