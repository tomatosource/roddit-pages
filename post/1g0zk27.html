Bronny James vs Eddie Bird

Over a decade after Larry Legend entered the league, his younger brother Eddie Bird gave it a try.

Eddie averaged 22 points as a High School Senior and followed in Larry’s footsteps attending Indiana State. Through 4 seasons he averaged 14 points, 3.7 rebounds, 1.6 assists with 43.4%/37.4%/83.5% shooting in 25.6mpg. Eddie went on to get drafted #12 of in the minor league USBL where he averaged 6.6 points in 8 appearances. Eventually, the Sacramento Kings gave him an 11 day contract, but he wasn’t quite good enough to earn a slot. Unfortunately, this was 1991 and his older brother was less than a year from retiring and wasn’t all that interested in nepoing his brother onto the Celtics and hooking him up with a guaranteed multi-million dollar contract. In-fact, Larry seemed fairly dismissive of the young pup publicly suggesting his lil bro was in for a wakeup call if he kept up his lackluster work ethic. Still, Eddie had decent size at 6’6 190 and a pretty decent shot - breaking some high school records and shooting 45% from three one year in College.

On the flip side you have Bronny James who averaged 14 points as a High School Senior, 4.8 points on 36%/26%/67% shooting in College, a dreadful 8 points on 35%/14% shooting in Summer League (ranking him outside the top 250 performances of that semi-pro showcase), and thus far a embarrassing 1-10 FG and 0-4 from three off his daddy’s bench in preseason.

One must ask, who is better between 6’1 Bronny and 6’6 Eddie. To be honest, it’s hard one to really give an answer to, because Eddie Bird hasn’t really been heard from since 1991 and if he’s still alive, there’s no telling what kind of shape the 58 year old is in, but my money is on Eddie Bird.
