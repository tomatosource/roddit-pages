Celtics’ INCREDIBLE Road Stats Since 21-22

Full disclosure: I am a Celtics fan (and yes, I made this same post over on r/bostonceltics). However, these are raw stats, they provide some context as the Finals shifts to Dallas, and they’re just remarkable numbers that no other team has come close to replicating in recent seasons.

It may be common knowledge that the Celtics are a good road team – especially in the playoffs, where they seem to be much better on the road than at home. But the numbers are really quite eye-opening when you dig in to them.

  1. Boston¬†has the most regular season road wins in the NBA¬†(75) since the start of the 21-22 season. That translates to about a 50-win pace over an 82 game season. They also have the most road wins¬†over just the last two seasons.
  2. Boston has, by far,¬†the most playoff road wins over the last 3 seasons (20). Their 20-7 record away from home is a .740 winning percentage – roughly a 61-win pace over 82 games. The next best team is Miami, with a record of 12-12. Third place? Dallas (10-11).
  3. Not only does Boston have the most road playoff wins during this stretch, they’re one of just three teams to even have a winning record. The others: Denver (9-8) and OKC (3-2).
  4. Even if you were to take away Boston’s 6-0 postseason record this season, they’d still be 14-7. In other words, if you completely disregarded their road performance this postseason…they’d still have the most road playoff wins and the best road winning percentage out of every team of the last three seasons.
  5. On that note, if you just¬†consider the last two postseasons, Boston’s road numbers are even more ridiculous: they’re 12-3 on the road – an 80% win rate. That equates to about 66 wins over a full season.
  6. Going back to the start of the 21-22 playoffs, Boston¬†has won at least one road game in every single series¬†(bar the Finals vs. Dallas, in which they have obviously yet to play a road game). Here’s the breakdown:

22 v Brooklyn: 2-0 on the road

22 v Milwaukee: 2-1

22 v Miami: 3-1

22 v Golden State: 1-2

23 v Atlanta: 2-1

23 v Philadelphia: 2-1

23 v Miami: 2-1

And then 2-0 against each of Miami, Cleveland, and Indiana this postseason

  1. As the above shows, not only has Boston won at least one road game in every series, they’ve actually had a¬†winning road record in every series except for the 22 Finals. On a personal note, this one is particularly bonkers to me.

All of this is to say, recent history suggests it will be very difficult for Dallas to win both game 3 and game 4.
