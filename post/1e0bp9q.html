A note on Embiid’s national team eligibility

It’s bothered me the discourse that there’s been about Embiid playing for the USA and felt the need to address some things as most NBA fans don’t follow international basketball that much.

First, I know rational people are fine with it and understand it’s his personal choice, yadda, yadda. But I understand people having their own opinions even if they acknowledge it’s his choice.

But now to address his options.

“He’s medal chasing, he should’ve played for France.”

Embiid is not French, has never lived in France, and has no french connection outside of Cameroon previously being a French territory so it’s a Francophone country. Embiid playing for France would’ve been “medal chasing.”

He gained French citizenship through naturalization as a person of “exceptional interest.” Embiid playing for France would be just like Rondae Hollis-Jefferson playing for Jordan, a player with no connection just naturalized simply to play basketball. FIBA rules allows countries to naturalize one player for their team. He is infinitely more American than he is French.

Why not Cameroon?

To frame things, fellow Cameroonian Pascal Siakam has also never played for Cameroon. Siakam has indicated he would love to but never has. Why hasn’t Siakam played for them despite being vocal about being open to it?

For people who don’t know, when NBA players play for their national teams, the country’s federation has to pay for insurance in case the player gets injured while on duty. If a player gets injured, they will have to pay their NBA salary while they’re injured and also potential damages to their career. And we all know how expensive NBA contracts are so the insurance would be pretty hefty for it.

It’s not unexpected for the less powerful basketball countries to not call up NBA players because they can’t afford to pay the insurance for their contract. There’s a ton of Nigerian (born and raised or by ancestry) NBA players but the best ones haven’t always played for them. I remember very clearly Festus Ezeli and Al-Farouq Aminu couldn’t play in one Olympics because Nigeria wouldn’t pay for the insurance. (Nigeria has seemed to be much more invested in recent years)

There’s previously been reports that Cameroon can’t afford Siakam. There’s also been other reasons thrown about but unless you think it’s purely posturing by Siakam saying he would, it would seem more to be that he can’t and that would be the reason why he can’t. And when you add in that Embiid makes more and is a much higher injury risk, I’d be stunned if they could afford it.

tl;dr not french, Cameroon almost certainly can’t pay his insurance
