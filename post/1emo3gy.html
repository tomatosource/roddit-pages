Former Kings and Heat guard Kyle Guy retires from basketball at age 26, after being the FIBA Champions League Top Scorer the past season, in order to return to his alma mater and serves as an athlete development mentor and special assistant for the Virginia Cavaliers

Source

Kyle Guy won the Virginia Cavaliers the NCAA title in 2019 and was named the Final Four Most Outstanding Player.

After bouncing out of the NBA after three seasons playing for the Kings and the Heat, he signed with Joventut Badalona of the Eurocup (second tier European continential club competition) and the Spanish league.

After playing well in both competitions, he got picked up by Euroleague giants Panathinaikos. He failed to adapt to the higher level of play and struggled mightily for the Greek club that season.

He went to back to Spain and signed with FIBA Champions League (third tier European international club competition) side Lenovo Tenerife on January 1st of this year. He ended up having the best season of his career with them, averaging 22 PPG on 62% TS, and leading his team to the FIBA Champions League final, which they lost to Unicaja. He was names the FIBA Champions League Top Scorer for that season, and put up an 18/6/4 statline in the final against Unicaja, being the game’s leading scorer.

He retired from basketball unexpectedly today in order to return to his alma mater and serve as an athlete devolopment mentor and special assistant.
