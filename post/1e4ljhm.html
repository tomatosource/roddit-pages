Kendrick Nunn won’t be returning to the NBA after an amazing season in Europe, since his NBA out clause has expired. Nunn will play for Greek giants Panathinaikos until at least 2026.

Source

Nunn was selected to the All-Euroleague First Team his rookie Euroleague season, averaging 15/3/3 on 54% TS (54⁄55 FT) during the regular season and 19/3/3 on 65% TS in the quarter-finals series against Maccabi (25 PTS in Game 3, 27 PTS in Game 4 and 26 PTS in Game 5, best of five series).

Panathinaikos ended up winning the Euroleague title after Nunn put up a 14/4/3 on 67% TS in the semi-finals against Fenerbahce, and 21/3/3 on 54% TS in the final against Real Madrid.

Panathinaikos also won the Greek league finals this year against Olympiacos, winning 3-2 after being down 0-2 in the series, with Nunn averaging 19/4/4 (21 PTS in Game 1 and Game 3, and 25 PTS in Game 4).

Nunn signed a two-year contract extension with Panathinaikos after the end of the quarter-finals series against Maccabi, which included an NBA out clause for this summer, but it has now expired so Nunn will spend the next two years playing for the club from Athens.
