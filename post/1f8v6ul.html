Why Wilt and Russell are the Only Unforgettable Players in 1960s?

Sometimes I wonder how amazing that Wilt and Russell’s Legacy are still on the running today.

50s Superstars are forgotten now. A casual nba fan will not know most superstars in 60s like Jerry or Oscar or Bob or Elgin. Unless he/she is interested in Nba history.

Heck, even 70s stars are forgotten now. Except for Kareem. (And Dr. J if you count ABA)

However, Even the most normal fans. Even the younger fans know Wilt and Russell.

I am in my Mid 20s. My father was not even alive when Wilt/Russell played. But I still know them like I know Modern Nba players.

Even after 60 years, Wilt and Russell’s legacy remains to be in the heart and minds of modern people.

Why is that?

Is it just simply because of:

Bill Russell’s unbreakable 11 rings as the best player AND Wilt Chamberlain’s 100 points in a game, and 50 ppg in a season?
