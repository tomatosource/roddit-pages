Do you expect Immanuel Quickley to live up to his 5 year, 175 million dollar extension?

The Toronto Raptors signed Immanuel Quickley to a 5 yr/$175M extension last month. He was a RFA this summer but never was truly under threat from other teams and the Raptors locked IQ up to a mega deal along with Scottie Barnes to be the faces of their franchise and their future.

Quickley was the prize acquisition in the Anunoby trade last season for the Raptors and though Toronto struggled after the trade, IQ played decent ball. He’s a fantastic shotmaker and historically is a great defender. He’s coming along as a playmaker, had a career high assist rate overall, was around 30% with the Raptors, and his turnover rate remained fairly stable. He had shown flashes of playmaking ability with the Knicks and showed with the Raptors he could potentially be a legit QB of a team.

His runner has become a staple of his game but wasn’t great with the Raptors. He has been a good finisher at the rim but relies a lot on the runner or bank shots. He shot 45% from 2 with the Raptors last season but the team was in a flux with injuries surrounding him. It was also his first time starting and being at the top of a team’s scouting report.

Do you expect IQ to grow into the contract he received from Toronto? He isn’t a star yet, but the Raptors are banking on him to become one. What do you all think?
