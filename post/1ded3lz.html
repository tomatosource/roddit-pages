These are in the last 10 games, playoffs included. I think there is some value to these stats considering Tatum played 3 playoff series without KP, so there is some sample size for how he plays without him, even if it’s not against Dallas so far. Obviously the stakes are much higher and the Mavs defense has done a great job against Tatum, but

From an eye test perspective, it always looked like Brown and Porzingis played better off of each other as opposed to Tatum and KP. KP shoots a lot of his 3’s from the top of the 3 and from similar positions where Tatum is. And when KP is on the floor, Tatum is more of a guard instead of getting into the post which is where he improved greatly this season, both from a scoring and facilitating standpoint. Obviously Tatum has not shot well over the course of the playoffs and even though he shot 3⁄7 from 3 in game 1, he is going to have to either change his shot selection or his approach to 3’s in this game. The pull-ups seem to never go in, but he is better when the ball moves to him around the perimeter.

Tatum has historically been pretty dependable in road games and he is also a very solid bounce-back player, as in he tends to have good games after dogshit shooting performances (which, lmao I guess game 2 could have been a bounce back from the bad shooting in game 1 but at least he cut his turnovers by half and doubled his assists).
