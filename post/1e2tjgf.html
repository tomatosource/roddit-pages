Jerry Stackhouse came 4th in MIP Voting after averaging 29.8 PPG the Previous Season

In the 2000-2001 season, Jerry Stackhouse averaged 29.8 ppg. The next season, from 2001-2002, he averaged 21.3 seasons and came 4th in MIP voting. Although he didn’t earn that many points in the voting process, it’s still very interesting to see any player receive significant placement within MIP voting after their PPG drops by 8 pts.

I’m not sure how accurate this is, because of just how unlikely this situation is. I tried to search for other sources to corroborate this, in case this was a bug in Basketball Reference, but I couldn’t find anything. In this aspect, feedback would be highly appreciated.
