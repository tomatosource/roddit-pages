What do non-Lakers fans think about the Lakers decision to essentially keep the same team for next season?

I am curious to see what Non-Lakers fans think about the Lakers front office deciding to essentially run the same roster back from last season?

The only major changes were as follows:

Firing head coach Darvin Ham and his coaching staff.

Replacing Ham and his coaching staff with JJ Redick as the next head coach and JJ’s new coaching staff that has Nate McMillan, Scott Brooks, Lindsey Harding, Greg St Jean and Beau Lebesque.

Replacing Taurean Prince and Spencer Dinwiddie with two drafted players in Dalton Knecht and Bronny James.

Extending LeBron James and Max Christie.

D’angelo Russell, Jaxson Hayes, Christian Wood and Cam Reddish opting into their player options to stay on the team for the upcoming season.

I belive the Lakers depth chart will be as below:

PG: D’lo, Gabe, JHS, Bronny SG: Reaves, Christie, Max Lewis SF: Rui, Cam Reddish, Dalton Knecht PF: LeBron, Vanderbilt, Wood C: AD, Hayes, Wood

I personally think this team is no where near good enough towards being a legit contender. I view us as finishing anywhere between 6-11, with 7th (just at the end of the regular season, not considering play-in games determining seeding), as my predicted spot with this current roster.

I think the Lakers should have made moves over the offseason to try to improve the team, but the front office think the new coaching staff and better health will improve the team to be successful throughout the regular season.

I do think the Lakers will make moves at the trade deadline.

For this team to be a legit contender in the playoffs, D’lo cannot be the Lakers starting point guard. He is way too inconsistent and streaky to be a reliable starting point guard in the playoffs.

The D’lo-Reaves back court is also abysmal defensively and is not good enough offensively to justify this awful defensive pairing in the playoffs. Reaves has been great and reliable in the playoffs, unlike D’lo, who is also an expiring contract after this upcoming season, so I think he should be traded by the deadline at the latest.

I also think the team lacks a sufficient back up C for AD. Hayes is solid and hopefully he can improve this season, but I still don’t think he will be good enough to be a team with contending hopes main back up C.

The Lakers should try trade for another C sometime before the trade deadline, to give AD sufficient help and allow him to rest more during regular season and even playoff games if he can.

I know that the team has tried to make trades but there apparently haven’t been any great offers so far.

But I think the front office has not been greatly determined to search for a good deal and are happy to keep the same roster.

I think the Lakers front offices lack of desire to search for a trade is the main reason they could not find anything.

I think this is the case because other teams that consider themselves as contending teams have been able to execute trades to upgrade their roster, such as OKC trading Giddey for Caruso and the Knicks trading for Mikal.
