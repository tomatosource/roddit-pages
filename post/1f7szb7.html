Are only 8 teams in the running to make the Eastern Conference playoffs?

Last year’s Eastern Conference standings:

Seed Team Record
1 Boston Celtics 64-18
2 New York Knicks 50-32
3 Milwaukee Bucks 49-33
4 Cleveland Cavaliers 48-34
5 Orlando Magic 47-35
6 Indiana Pacers 47-35
7 Philadelphia 76ers 47-35
8 Miami Heat 46-36
9 Chicago Bulls 39-43
10 Atlanta Hawks 36-46
11 Brooklyn Nets 32-50
12 Toronto Raptors 25-57
13 Charlotte Hornets 21-61
14 Washington Wizards 15-67
15 Detroit Pistons 14-68

Of course, the nature of the play-in’s sudden-death format means that even a 7-seed could fall out of the playoffs through injury or miraculous 3pt shooting, but it’s unlikely, and I think it much more likely that the current top 8 stay the top 8.

Purely considering these bottom 7 teams, they have stayed bad or become worse. The Bulls lost DeRozan, Atlanta lost Dejounte Murray, Brooklyn lost Mikal Bridges, Toronto lost both Anunoby and Siakam and the last three teams are so poorly run I doubt any of them crack 30 wins.

Considering the East playoffs teams of last year, the Celtics will likely stay atop the East standings due to their stacked roster, the Knicks loom dangerously with the addition of aforementioned Mikal Bridges, and the 76ers have acquired the third star that Tobias Harris was supposed to be in Paul George. The young teams of Orlando, Indiana and Cleveland will only improve, and the Bucks and the Heat, while starting to get long in the tooth, have good enough teams to get to a positive record, even with the potential decline of Lillard and Butler.

My question really is: Who of these 8 teams going to fall off a cliff hard enough to be worse than the Raptors or the Hawks?
