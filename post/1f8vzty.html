Derrick Rose and Wes Unseld are the only MVP winners to make only one All-NBA team in their careers.

Unseld’s career is interesting.

He was MVP, ROY, All-NBA 1st Team, and an All-Star in his rookie season, 1968-69, averaging 13.8 ppg and 18.2 rpg, after helping Baltimore improve by 21 wins over the previous season (from 36-46 to 57-25).

Then he finished 8th in MVP voting in his second season, but wasn’t named an All-Star or All-NBA, even though he had similar averages (16.2 ppg and 16.7 rpg) and his team had similar success (50-32).

He was an All-Star four more times in his career, and received MVP votes in three more seasons, but never made All-NBA again after year one.
