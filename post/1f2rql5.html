The Pistons could be way better than last year.

Significantly in this case meaning they could win 32 games but even that’s optimistic, expecting an 18 game swing out of that team is optimistic, but I do really believe that they can.

The Pistons had zero spacing last year and Cade struggled mightily for it, but I have to say that he handled it as well as you could as a leader for a team as bad as that year’s Pistons, but in theory, Harris, THJ, and Beasley should be able to make up for it and he’ll have a much better season.

Call those three chuckers or trash, they can at least play semi-competently over the course of a season, which should help spread the floor. The thought there is that one of Beas or THJ would try to prove themselves to be valuable to a contender and try to get themselves traded, while Tobi just chases the bag wherever it may be.

All the players that are part of that team’s future have a chance to improve to some degree, at least enough so that they don’t lose games just be existing on the basketball court. But the biggest culprits of that are gone, thankfully.

JB Bickerstaff is good at helming rebuilds and his time in the doldrums of Cleveland before they got good again shows that you can count on him to care about the team more than Monty did.

All in all, I’m very optimistic about the Pistons, haven’t seen enough or know about Ron Holland to say anything about him but it’s only two months until the season starts and we can judge them then.
