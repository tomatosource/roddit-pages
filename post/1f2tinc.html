Now that time has passed, what are your thoughts on the addition of DeMar DeRozan for the Sacramento Kings?

Time has passed, and training camps are due to open in about ~5 weeks. The season is approaching. Good time now to really analyze where teams are going into the season and different offseason moves.

DeMar DeRozan was on the market for a while, but ended up getting paid—3 years, roughly $74M, in a three team deal that sent Harrison Barnes to San Antonio and Chris Duarte to Chicago.

The Kings offense suffered the second half of the season with injuries to Fox, Huerter, Monk…they defeated the Warriors in their first play-in game as the 9th seed but went down quite meekly to the Pelicans in their second play-in game. The Kings had been looking to make an impact move for a while and were able to land DeRozan, without giving up significant future core pieces. They gave up a ‘31 unprotected pick swap to the Spurs but that is something we won’t know the value of for years down the line.

The injuries exposed some holes in their offensive attack last season. The Kings lacked another three level scorer outside of Fox and shot creation in general. Now enter DeRozan, who remains an elite midrange shotmaker and has become a good playmaker as well.

Some people have their questions about fit with their two stars in Fox and Sabonis. The Kings knew they needed to add talent to keep up in the tough West. Do you think DeMar was the right move to make and how much do you think he moves the needle for them? How good do you all think the fit is on this team? Do you expect DeMar to maintain his level of play as he hits his mid-30s?
