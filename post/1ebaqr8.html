[OC] I made a tier list for how tasty every NBA team is.

S Tier (Prime Cuts and Delicacies)

Milwaukee Bucks

  • Culinary Delicacy: Venison, the meat from deer, has a lean and rich flavor. This game meat contains little fat and offers a unique taste - sweet yet intense. It has a strong flavor makes it a controversial meat that some people absolutely hate and some people love. You can cook venison in lots of ways: as steaks, roasts, sausages, or in stews. Some cultures see venison as a special treat.

Chicago Bulls

  • Culinary Delicacy: Bulls belong to the cattle family, and their flesh known as beef, ranks among the world’s most popular and cherished proteins. People love beef for its adaptability, taste, and consistency, whether it’s juicy steaks, roasts hearty stews, or burgers. Various beef cuts, like ribeye, tenderloin, and brisket, provide a wide array of cooking options. The fat marbling in beef adds a rich and succulent quality making it a top choice for meat lovers.

A Tier (Game and Poultry)

Atlanta Hawks

  • Culinary Consideration: Hawks are birds of prey. While people don’t often eat them, they’re still birds, and you can eat birds. Folks enjoy game birds like quail, pheasant, and duck in different types of cooking. Hawks don’t show up on menus because of conservation and ethical concerns, not to mention it’s against the law. Still, the idea of eating hawks fits with the tradition of eating game birds, which people value for their special tastes and textures.

Charlotte Hornets

  • Culinary Consideration: Insects like hornets are consumed in various parts of the world. Entomophagy, or insect-eating, is a practice in many cultures, particularly in Asia and Africa, where insects are considered a nutritious and sustainable food source. Hornets can be roasted, fried, or even ground into powder for use in various dishes. They are high in protein and can be a crunchy and flavorful snack. Their larvae especially is considered tasty.

B Tier (Edible but Uncommon Choices/Taste Bad)

Toronto Raptors

  • Culinary Consideration: The term “Raptors” commonly refers to birds of prey, such as eagles, vultures, and condors. While not standard fare, these birds are technically edible. Raptors would offer a gamier flavor compared to more commonly consumed poultry. They likely don’t taste very good.

New Orleans Pelicans

  • Culinary Consideration: Pelicans are large water birds, and while not commonly eaten, birds are generally considered edible. The culinary use of pelican meat is extremely rare and not well-documented, possibly due to conservation status and ethical considerations. The idea of eating pelicans is more a theoretical consideration rather than a practical or cultural one. Pelicans also have been said to be not tasty by people who have tried them because they are carnivores.

Memphis Grizzlies

  • Culinary Consideration: Grizzlies, like other bears, are technically edible, but their meat is generally tough and greasy. The flavor can vary significantly based on the bear’s diet, with some bear meat being described as gamey and fishy, especially if the bear has a diet rich in fish. Bear meat also has a high fat content, which can affect its texture and taste.

Minnesota Timberwolves

  • Culinary Consideration: Wolves are canines, and while not commonly consumed in Western diets, they are technically edible. The meat of wolves is said to be tough, stringy, greasy, and gamey, similar to other wild game animals, but considered slightly less tasty. In some Indigenous cultures, consuming wolf meat is part of traditional practices, though it is not widely accepted or popularized in modern cuisine because it really doesn’t taste good.

C Tier (Edible in Theory)

Los Angeles Lakers, Sacramento Kings, Golden State Warriors, Cleveland Cavaliers, Washington Wizards, Dallas Mavericks, Portland Trail Blazers, Boston Celtics

  • Culinary Consideration: These team names are based on humans or human professions (e.g., Lakers, Kings, Warriors, Cavaliers). While humans are edible in theory, it is illegal to eat humans and they likely don’t taste good.

Philadelphia 76ers

  • Culinary Consideration: 76ers refers to the signers of the declaration of independence in 1776. Humans are technically edible, but it might be hard to eat a bunch of dead people, given that time travel has yet to be invented.

D Tier (Abstract or Inedible Concepts)

Miami Heat, Orlando Magic, Phoenix Suns, Utah Jazz, Oklahoma City Thunder

  • Culinary Consideration: These names represent abstract concepts, natural phenomena, or inanimate entities that are not edible. For example, “Heat” refers to temperature, “Magic” to mystical arts, “Suns” to celestial bodies, “Jazz” to a genre of music, and “Thunder” to a sound. They can’t be eaten since they’re abstract. I don’t know where else to put them though, so I put them here.

F Tier (Inedible Objects)

Denver Nuggets

  • Culinary Consideration: The “Nuggets” in Denver Nuggets could refer to gold nuggets, which are obviously not edible. However, if interpreted as chicken nuggets, it would be edible, assuming the Nuggets aren’t from McDonalds. But, given the situation, it’s clearly referring to gold nuggets, which aren’t consumable without purification.

New York Knicks, Indiana Pacers, Brooklyn Nets, Detroit Pistons, Los Angeles Clippers, Houston Rockets, San Antonio Spurs

  • Culinary Consideration: These names refer to objects or items that are clearly inedible. For instance, “Knicks” are named after knickerbockers, a type of pants; “Pacers” to a type of gait popular in horse harness racing; “Nets” to basketball nets or fishing nets; “Pistons” to engine parts; “Clippers” to ships; “Rockets” to spacecraft; and “Spurs” to riding gear. These items are not only inedible but also often composed of materials that are toxic if ingested. Maybe there’s a chance of pants being edible, but none of the other ones.
