The 76ers have 15 back-to-back games in the 2024⁄25 season. If Embiid plans to not play any back-to-back games this season, he can only miss 2 games to qualify for MVP or All-NBA consideration.

This is due to the new CBA where, aside from Rookie of the Year, the yearly awards require players to play 65 of the 82 games encompassing the regular season, ergo they can only miss 17 games of a given season. If they fail to meet this threshold, they are disqualified for those awards i.e. All-NBA, MVP, DPoY, and so on. The main exceptions to this involve Rookie of the Year and the All-Rookie Team awards.

For the record, none of those back-to-backs are in Denver, which he has not played in since November 8th, 2019.

If you pencil in the yearly away game against the Nuggets as another guaranteed missed game, he can only miss one game before he is disqualified.
