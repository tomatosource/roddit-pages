On December 19, 2007, Shawn Marion played in two NBA games for two different teams … technically

On December 19, 2007, the Miami Heat played the Atlanta Hawks. Originally, the Hawks won 117-111, but it was soon realized that Shaquille O’Neal was incorrectly ejected after receiving five fouls (ejections are supposed to happen after six).

The remainder of the game was replayed three months later on March 8th, 2008 beginning with 0:59 left in the 4th quarter and the Hawks up 114-111. No points were scored and the Hawks won 114-111.

The twist? Shaquille O’Neal was traded for Shawn Marion before the replay took place. So, per NBA records, Shawn Marion technically played for 52 seconds in the December 19th, 2007 Hawks vs Heat game, while also playing in the December 19th, 2007 Suns vs Mavericks game.

If you’re wondering, it does show up that way on his game log:

https://i.imgur.com/epN8T7M.png

Also, another fun tidbit is that the replay was scheduled to occur right before the next game between those two teams. So the Hawks and Heat finished the last minute of their December game, with the Hawks winning (without scoring, mind you). And then directly afterward, the teams started a whole new game - which the Hawks won again. The Hawks celebrated two victories over the Heat in one day.
