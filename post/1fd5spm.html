The 1991 Bulls and the 1998 Bulls never lost a playoff game by more than one possession

The 1991 Bulls lost two games in their championship run, a 2-point loss to the Sixers in the ECSF, and a 2-point loss in Game 1 of the 1991 NBA Finals.

The 1998 Bulls lost 6 games in the playoffs - 1 to the Hornets, 3 to the Pacers, 2 to the Jazz. Never by more than 3 points.

Even teams that destroyed the playoffs like the 83 Sixers, 2001 Lakers, and 2017 Warriors had bigger loss margins. To always be within one possession is insane - to do it twice is unbelievable.

1991 is super impressive because Jordan and Pippen had played all 82 games and had never made a title run before - to have that consistency in the playoffs is remarkable.

1998 is arguably even more impressive - to not have a single off night where you lose convincingly, when you are jaded from attempting to complete a 2nd three peat in 8 years, and in Jordan’s case when you’ve played all 82 games of the regular season yet again, incredible.
