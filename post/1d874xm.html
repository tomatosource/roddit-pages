Regardless of the NBA finals outcome, this will be the longest period of parity since 1975 - 1980

From 1975-1980 no franchise in the NBA won more than one championship, leading to six unique winners consecutively: Warriors, Celtics, Trailblazers, Bullets, Sonics, and Lakers.

That trend stopped in 1981 when Larry Bird and the Celtics won the NBA championship. From then until 2019, the longest period of parity was only four years, however in the past five championships no team has won twice (Raptors, Lakers, Bucks, Warriors, Nuggets).

Thus, regardless of the outcome of this year’s finals, this six year period will be tied as the longest period of parity in the NBA!
