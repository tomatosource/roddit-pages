LeBron James becomes the first player with multiple triple-doubles in the Olympics

With 2:37 in the fourth quarter against Serbia, LeBron James grabbed his tenth rebound of the game. Thirteen seconds later, he had an assist to Steph Curry, who made a 3 that gave the U.S. an 87–86 lead. That assist was his tenth of the game, making LeBron the only player to record more than one triple-double in the Olympics, unless the article I read disregarded women to have done it. Here are the other times a triple-double has been recorded in Olympic play:

  • Sasha Belov, USSR: 23 points, 14 rebounds and 10 assists against Canada in 1976
  • LeBron: 11 points, 14 rebounds and 12 assists versus Australia in 2012
  • Luka Doncic, Slovenia: 16 points, 10 rebounds and 18 assists against France in 2021
