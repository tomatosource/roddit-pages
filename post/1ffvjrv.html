Is individual defense overrated?

I see a lot of the time in nba discourse where the vast majority of fans believe a player’s offense and defense hold the same value, or 50⁄50. I dont think this is true at all. I believe offense is about 70-80% of a player’s value compared to their defense.

It seems pretty simple to me. It’s must easier to prevent a great defensive player from impacting the game than a great offensive player. Avoiding defensive players is pretty simple, whereas keeping the ball out of one offensive player is insanely difficult.

And this is especially the case in the context of perimeter players. Unlike rim protectors who will pretty much always be influencing multiple players as the first helper, perimeter players much more rarely provide help defense. So they are limited to affecting 1⁄5 of the players while on offense they can create numerous advantages.

This isnt to understate the value of team defense of course. A team’s defense is historically much more necessary in winning championships than their offense. But people claiming “x player is better than y because there are two sides to basketball” even though offense is much more valuable needs to stop. I think jokic being the best player in the league has started opening eyes to this, but even still many people still blindly say embiid or giannis are better solely because of defense.
