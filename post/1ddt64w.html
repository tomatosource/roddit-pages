My encounter with a current Celtic in their teens.

My encounter with a young Celtic.

[This is longer, but worth it when I tell it too friends]

When I was a teen, years ago, I was a Boy Scout. Troop 6 Cranston (“Best by far”). Eventually made Eagle Scout. In my third year I was “Troop Guide,” a leadership position within the unit where an older scout teaches new scouts basic skills.

Two new scouts were having trouble setting up a tent. I went over to show them how, as it was quite difficult the first time without instruction.

As I finish, and hunch over on all fours to go inside and smooth the bottom canvas, my butt is hanging out the front flap.

I hear one the kids make a frrp fart noise and say, “dang someone is farting,” while giggling.

I come out of the tent, stare at the kid, and say “really, I’m trying to help you, man.”

I go back in the tent, all fours, butt still hanging out. frrp. Kid jokes again, “Man, what you have to eat.”

I back out, look at the kid and say, “have fun setting the fly (the rain guard on top of the tent) yourself.”

As I turn, my back (and butt) are facing the kid. As I step away, a final frrp. I almost cuff him. I forget about this for decades.

Last summer, I am sitting around a fire at Camp Yawgoog with some oldhead scout friends, talking Celtics, the Heat series, JBs new 300mm contract. One of the guys says, “crazy that Coach Mazzulla was in Troop 6 for about a month before quitting.”

It all floods back - Crazy Joe mocked me with fart noises back around 1999 or 2000.

Hand to god, this is a true story, and probably 95% verbatim (25 years is a long time). I think it is hilarious in retrospect.

Nothing but love for Joe. He gave me a great anecdote. Guess he had that iconoclast fire in him from a young age. You need that dog in you to represent the bench where the Secretary of Defense, Red, The Legend, the Truth, Doc, Chief, Tommy and Houdini sat. No hate for coach Mazz from me.

But I will never help that f***er set up a tent again.

Go Celty Boys, win that second banner in my adult life.
