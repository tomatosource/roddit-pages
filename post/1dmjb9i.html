What role player do you think would benefit most from being 2 inches taller and 10 lbs heavier while maintaining their current athleticism and skillset?

This is something I’ve thought about a bit over the years as I feel the Celtics have had a fair few guys in my time watching the team where I’d think to myself, “If he were just a tad bigger I think he’d be a lot more valuable.” I’d say the main player I’ve thought this about would be Grant Williams.

If he were 6’8 instead of 6’6 I think teams would attempt to use him as a smallball 5 a lot more often because of his combination of outside shooting, strength, and BBIQ. His playing more at the Center spot would also allow him to playmake more out of the short roll which is a skillset of his that I think has gone underutilized. Lastly it’d be far more forgivable for him to be as inconsistent a perimeter defender as he is if he were a 5 rather than as a 4.

My next choice would be Daniel Theis. He’s tough, physical, can defend on the perimeter and is a very heady player on offense. He’s basically a smaller Al Horford without the 3 point shot, but said lack of size really hurts him as a Center. If he were 6’10, 255 lbs instead of 6’8, 245 I think he’d have ended up as a starter in the NBA instead of being primarily a bench rotation piece.

Any players that come to mind for you guys?
