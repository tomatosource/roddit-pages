Vince Carter’s Illustrious Campaign with the Toronto Raptors | Simplified

Vince Carter has done a lot for the Raptors, like dunking the ball, but here’s some other fun facts you probably didn’t know about:

Skipped his predraft workout with the Raptors only to be traded there on draft night anyway.

Chose to go to a graduation ceremony the morning of Game 7 against the 76ers so he could have an excuse for losing later that day.

Called his whole team easily predictable on offense.

Has a funny quote on his top 10 quotes of all time:

“I don’t care how I got here. In the books, when you look at it 10 or 20 years from now, it’s not going say how he got here, it’s going to say he’s here and he represented the team.” Source

Also…

Vince Carter couldn’t crack a 50 win season in his tenure:

1998-1999: 23-27 (Lockout Year)

1999-2000: 45-37 (Lost in Rd 1 vs Knicks)

2000-2001: 47-35 (Lost in Rd2 in game 7 to the Sixers; also missed the buzzer beater)

2001-2002: 42-40 (Rd 1 loss to the Pistons)

2002-2003: 24-58 (Halfman/Halfglass season)

2003-2004: 33-49 (Beginning of Quitter Season)

For a guy paraded for what he brought to the city he really had a lackluster career in Canada.
