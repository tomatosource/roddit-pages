It’s amazing how NBA history is written and partitioned so neatly into decades.

What I mean is the 60s (decade of Celtics and Lakers) literally starts with the Celtics winning in 1960 and ends with in a 1969 classic Lakers vs Celtics featuring all the 60s legends Chamberlain, Balyor, West, Russell, Havlicek, with the Celtics winning.

The 70s (decade of parity and KAJ) literally starts with the Knicks then KAJ winning. And ends with it featuring a Finals in 1979 with two teams and player’s representative of parity (no real superstars and two small market teams that aren’t dominant franchises/dynasties).

The 80s, without missing a beat, starts with Bird Magic being drafted and Magic/Lakers winning in the first year of the decade. The rest of the decade is history, with the Lakers or Celtics featured every single year.

The 90s (91, cheating a bit but close enough) starts with none other than the Bulls/Jordan beating the old 80s Lakers dynasty, seriously can this be any more well written?

The 90s ends and 2000s starts with Shaq/Kobe/Duncan (without a doubt the top 3 2000s players) all winning their first titles and ends with one of them winning as well.

The 2010s decade again starts (ok 2011, cheating a bit here) with Lebron making his second and first amongst his 8 consecutive finals and ends with Lebron winning.

The 2020s (seems like it will be the age of parity again) starts with 5 different teams winning.

No insight, just a bit amazed how neatly these decades fall into place. And yes there’s a lot of ‘historians writing a story’ bias but I mean it’s still quite amazing imo.
