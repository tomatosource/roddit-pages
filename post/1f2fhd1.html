Bruno Caboclo will practice with the Warriors until Thursday, with hopes of receiving a contract offer

Source

Caboclo’s agent Daniel Hazan stated this in an interview with an Israeli media oulet.

“He will train with the Golden State Warriors until Thursday with the aim of signing a contract, and he believes he will succeed,” Hazan told Sports Channel in Israel.

Caboclo also received a contract offer from Hapoel Tel Aviv (reportedly €1.700.000 per season post-taxes), the team which Patrick Beverley also signed for this summer, to play in the Eurocup (second tier continential European club competition) this season.

“We are in negotiations, we are making good progress. It’s definitely an option worth keeping,” Hazan said.

Hapoel Tel Aviv made some big moves this summer, also signing the starting center of Euroleague powerhouse Fenerbahce Jonathan Motley, Ish Wainwright from the NBA, Lithuanian league Finals MVP Marcus Foster, All-FIBA Champions League (third tier European continential club competition) First Team selection and assists leader Joe Ragland, Ben Bentil etc.

Caboclo played for Partizan Belgrade in the Euroleague last season. He’s had a dispute with the club ever since he unexpectedly left the country a couple days prior to the start of the Serbian league finals series. He’s been fined and isn’t counted on to play for Partizan next season, but is still under contract. The dispute between Partizan and Caboclo’s agent is still going on.

Caboclo averaged 9 PTS and 4 REB on 73% TS in the Euroleague last season.

He’s played amazing at both the Olympic Qualifying Tournament in Riga (18 PTS, 7 REB, 2 BLK on 78% TS) and at the Paris Olympics (17 PTS, 7 REB, 2 STL, 1 BLK on 66% TS - 33⁄17 on 79% TS vs Japan, 30⁄9 on 70% TS va the US).

Caboclo’s played in the NBA in the past, with the Raptors, Kings, Grizzlies and Rockets.
