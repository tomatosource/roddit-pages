Will LeBron surpass Nat Hickey as the oldest player to ever play in an NBA game?

What LeBron has done is amazing and he has shown no signs of slowing down. Seeing how he’s entering his age 40 season, I’ve decided to see if he could possibly become the oldest player to ever play in an NBA game.

Right now, that honor goes to Nat Hickey, who was a player/coach in 1948 at the age of 45. According to Wikipedia, and NBA.com, he checked into a game against the Knicks, missed his only field goal attempt and committed four personal fouls.

LeBron is only a few years away from Nat and has the advantage of modern exercise science, nutrition and physical therapy. He also plays in an era of load management, so he can sit out games whenever he needs rest. Also, he might want to hold on for Bryce to get to the league, so he can be the first guy to play with two of his sons in the NBA.

I think he can do it. What do you guys thinks?
