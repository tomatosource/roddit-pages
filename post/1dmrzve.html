[OC] The interesting case of the 2009-2010 Charlotte Bobcats.

Introduction

The 2009-2010 Charlotte Bobcats are a very interesting team. It’s not likely you remember that team, so let me refresh your memory. Their main players were Gerald Wallace, Boris Diaw, Stephen Jackson, and Raymond Felton. Tyson Chandler was also on that team, but he was injured for a big part of the year.

About that team

Now, Gerald “Crash” Wallace was an absolutely sensational defender at his prime. He was a master at getting blocks and he was a great rim defender. He also drew a lot of free throws and went to the rim a lot. Wallace was also the most efficient player on the team besides Tyson Chandler, with a +4.3 rTS%. Stephen Jackson was the main scorer on the team with an inflation adjusted total of 22 points per 75, but on a dreadful -2.3 rTS%. Boris Diaw and Raymond Felton were also good players on the team, especially Felton who had the most assists per 75 possessions with 7.5. With DJ Augustin, Ronald Murray, and Nazr Mohammed coming off the bench, the Hornets had a competent team. Those Hornets finished with the BEST defensive rating that year with a relative defensive rating of -4.8 in the 95th percentile all time. But they also had the 24th best offense with a -3.2 which is in the 15th percentile all time. Larry Brown is truly something else. What a coach!

What changed from the previous year.

The previous year, they had the 7th best defense, so it wasn’t completely out of the blue, but given their only real star was Wallace, it was probably a surprise to many. They didn’t even make very many changes in the offseason, the only change of note was trading Emeka Okafar for Tyson Chandler. That trade was heavily criticized at the time, for good reason. Okafar was one of their key players on offense, and their offense was already bad enough, so the trade seemed stupid. They made a midseason trade getting Stephen Jackson for Raja Bell.

What happened after/how things collapsed.

Unfortunately, that team collapsed the next year after letting their starting point guard Raymond Felton go and replacing him with Shaun Livingston. They gave away Tyson Chandler for free to save money. The Hornets went 34-48 a year after having the best defense in the league and they decided to rebuild and that was that. And just another year after that, they infamously went 7-59. What a tragic story indeed.
