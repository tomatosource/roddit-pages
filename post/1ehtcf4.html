The Hawks are building around Trae. How would you grade their offseason and what do you think their outlook is going forward?

Hawks went from 10th in the lottery to 1st in the draft and took Risacher. Risacher didn’t play much in Summer League but what we saw is a guy who is a plug-and-play guy who isn’t afraid to shoot, can get out in transition, and defend multiple positions.

The Hawks have built a roster around Trae most similar to their 2021 ECF team than we’ve seen since. Capela is still on the roster, but the Hawks reportedly have a high price on Capela. Okongwu has had injuries and although Capela has declined, he is still a serviceable C with good chemistry with Trae.

Jalen Johnson is definitely starting to come along but he too has had injuries. He fills the stat sheet, has become a solid wing defender. He doesn’t have an elite offensive skill yet but shows flashes and can contribute in all departments. Nice to have connectors and guys who have feel and can pass like Risacher and Johnson next to Trae.

Dyson Daniels has good feel for the game on both ends, and can guard. The shooting hasn’t come in for him yet, turnover rate is too high, but he is a smart cutter and talented passer and has some post game. If the shooting ever comes in he’d be a nice fit next to Trae. He’s still only 21. Bufkin gonna get real time this season and is an intriguing talent.

We’ll see what they do with Nance, but he’s the kind of high energy defensive minded forward that also would be a nice fit with the identity and roster it looks like the Hawks are looking to build around Trae if they keep him. If not, they’ll get maybe a few seconds for him and the assets don’t hurt. They’d trade Hunter if they could, but he doesn’t have much value. Not the worst player to have around though…his shot diet was more efficient last season, shot a career high from 3, but is also injury prone. With more team size, maybe he isn’t forced to play as much PF.

The Hawks got a first rounder in the 2025 Draft in the DeJounte deal (Lakers) and a decent chance at two first round picks this season…Kings have to make the playoffs for that to convey. Not unlikely they end up with two top 20-22 picks. They also gained a 2027 first. Wanna know what you all think about their offseason moves and if you think the direction they’ve taken is the right one for their franchise now and long term…or not.
