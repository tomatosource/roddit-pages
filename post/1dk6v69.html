Bol Bol, JT Thor, Thon Maker, Wenyen Gabriel and Carlik Jones named to South Sudan’s 25-man extended roster for the Paris Olympics. This will be South Sudan’s first ever Olympic appearance at basketball.

Source

It’s two week old news, but it hasn’t been posted anywhere, so I figured why not.

South Sudan played at their first ever World Cup last year, after finishing the qualifiers with a 5-1 record (four blowout wins), where they put up a great performance, finishing higher placed than any other African team (Angola, Egypt, Cape Verde and Ivory Coast), thus earning an Olympic berth straight away.

They beat China by 20 points in the group stage, lost to Serbia and failed to qualify for the next stage because of a narrow overtime loss to Puerto Rico. They finished the tournament in 17th place after big wins against the Phillipines (19 point win margin) and Angola (23 point win margin) in the classification rounds.

They achieved this without Bol Bol, JT Thor or Thon Maker on the squad.

South Sudan will be playing in the same group as the US, Serbia and the winner of the Olympic Qualifying Tournament in Puerto Rico (Lithuania, Italy, Puerto Rico, Mexico, Ivory Coast or Bahrein).
