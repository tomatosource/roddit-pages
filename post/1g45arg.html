NBA in UAE: Sportswashing Amid War Crimes & Human Rights Issues

As a key player in soft power, the UAE has engaged in various fields to boost its international influence. The Emirates’ soft power majorly reflects in its strategic engagement in global sports events. In its pattern of investing heavily in high-profile sport tournaments and teams, the UAE has become the hub for the National Basketball Association (NBA) in the Middle East.

The league marked its third trip to Abu Dhabi for the preseason games, in October 2024. The event started with the Boston Celtics’ Jaylen Brown greeting the Etihad Arena in Arabic. Despite the festive atmosphere, many noticed the UAE’s superficial display of modernization and tolerance. There have been ethical concerns regarding human rights violations under the UAE’s authoritarian regime.

NBA previously faced scrutiny for its partnerships with repressive regimes, including China and Rwanda. The league was now facing questions for choosing the UAE, a government that limits freedom of speech. Critics argued that NBA became another high-profile sports event to act as the UAE’s sportswashing tool, diverting attention from the Emirates’ controversial actions and enhancing its international reputation.
