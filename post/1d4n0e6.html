The Dallas Mavericks are the first team to beat three 50-win teams en route to the Finals since the 2010 Lakers

The Mavs beat the 51-win Clippers (though without Kawhi), 57-win Thunder, and 56-win Wolves on their way to the Finals. The last time a team beat three 50-win teams within their own conference was the Lakers in 2010, who also happened to face a Celtics superteam in the Finals. (The Heat were on pace to do this in 2020 if the season wasn’t shortened, as the 45-28 Pacers they beat in the first round would be on pace for 51 wins.)

For comparison, the Jokic-era Nuggets have not beaten a single 50-win team in the playoffs, and only two if you’re generous and give them shortened season records (2020 Jazz and Clippers were on pace for 50). Giannis’ Bucks have beaten one 50-win team (the 2021 Suns) and another if you include extrapolated records (2021 Nets). Embiid’s 76ers have not beaten a single one, extrapolations included. Add in the Mavs’ win against the Suns in 2022, and be generous and count all the shortened seasons, and Luka has as many wins against 50-win teams as the last 6 MVPs combined.
