Half the league is in the luxury tax. Here’s by how much.

Per Keith Smith, this is how the 2024⁄25 season is shaping up salary-wise: https://twitter.com/KeithSmithNBA/status/1817707681681125867

In order of most to least “over the line” luxury tax teams:

Phoenix Suns: $52.8 million

Minnesota Timberwolves: $34.7 million

Boston Celtics: $27.8 million

Milwaukee Bucks: $22.3 million

Los Angeles Lakers: $17.4 million

Miami Heat: $13.9 million

Denver Nuggets: $11.8 million

Philadelphia 76ers: $6.5 million

Golden State Warriors: $5.8 million

Dallas Mavericks: $3.4 million

Los Angeles Clippers: $2.5 million

New York Knicks: $2.4 million

Cleveland Cavaliers: $2.1 million

New Orleans Pelicans: $1.4 million

Atlanta Hawks: $706,000

Obviously some of this can and likely will change, potentially in this very offseason. As an example: It’s hard to believe, for one, that Atlanta won’t cut salary given how close they are to getting under the threshold and the Pelicans already made some salary cutting moves last seasons such as when they dumped Kira Lewis Jr. as part of the Pascal Siakam trade.

Note that this is the luxury tax line and not the aprons; the first apron starts around $8 million above the luxury tax and carries with it a ton of different penalties to teambuilding, namely with regards to restrictions on trades.
