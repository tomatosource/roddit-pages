The Winningest NBA Players (OC)

I feel like I hear somewhat often about the “winningest” coaches (I’m still not convinced winningest is a word, but it’s what we’ve got), but almost never about players. Sometimes, we’ll hear about it with like quarterbacks or starting pitchers (though a “win” in baseball is somewhat more specific, statistically speaking), but never about who has won the most games while playing basketball. So I decided to figure it out. Now there are a few different ways to define “winningest.” You can either say the player who has won the most total games in his career, or you can say the player with the best winning percentage in his career. Both seem like valid measurements. (And since both seem like valid measurements, it’s why we have the Jordan/LeBron rift.)

I went through my top 120 players, which covers basically everyone who you could consider a “star” at one point or another, and figured out their regular season and postseason win-loss records. (I plan on doing this for everyone I track going forward, but it’s not as important or interesting to know which role players were on good teams for this discussion.) Note that these records only include games where a player actually saw playing time. If he was a DNP for any reason, the win (or loss) will not count toward his record. Minimums here are 400 regular season games, or 25 playoff games.

For the playoff numbers, keep in mind that this is just the raw data. At some point I’d like to go in and figure out when players missed the playoffs and just automatically give them four losses for the year (or however many games a first-round sweep was at the time). I did not do that here, yet, so players are not penalized for missing the playoffs. That doesn’t really matter for total wins, but just remember that it’s technically worse for a guy to eke his way into the playoffs and get swept, rather than to just not make the playoffs at all, when it comes to the career winning percentages.
