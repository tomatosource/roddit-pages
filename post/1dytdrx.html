[OC] Terry Rozier of the Miami Heat is Mr. Average, the most mid player of the 2023-24 season. Trayce Jackson-Davis is Mr. Extraordinary, the least average player of the 2023-24 season.

The NBA has official regular season awards for MVP and DPOY, denoting the most impactful players in the league (alongside awards like All-NBA and All-Defense). Haters on social media have also concocted up awards for Least Valuable Player. But what about the most average player in the league? Not so good that he needs to be an All-Star, and not so bad that he deserves slander. So I decided to find that.

The Methodology:

  • Using BasketballReference.com, compile the list of player statistics, per 100 possessions. I use 100 possessions rather than per game here, as I want to find the player with the most average impact, not the most average output. Otherwise, minutes played will be a factor.

  • Remove all players that played less than 58 games (70% of the regular season). This is the league minimum for statistical awards. I did consider other minimums as well, but decided against it. For example, I could have considered adding the 82 three-pointers made minimum as well, but that would exclude bad three-point shooters, which would skew the averages.

  • Determine the statistical categories you want to use. I decided on the following eight: PTS, TRB, AST, STL, BLK, FG%, 3P%, FT%. These are the five main box score stats and the three main shooting splits. I did consider using TS% instead of the three shooting splits, but a. that was a little extra work as it’s on a different page and b. how they got their efficiency definitely matters in determining the average.

  • Evaluate the median and standard deviation for each stat, per position. Although basketball is increasingly positionless, I noticed that doing it positionless would essentially mean the least average players would always be non-three point shooting centers and the most average players would always be wings. This way, you can find out who the most and least average players are based on the role they’re expected to fill.

  • Determine the player’s standard score in each category and add all the absolute values of those standard scores up.

The Results:

Terry Rozier of the Miami Heat is the most average player of the 2023-24 NBA season. He has a bit more points per possession than the average PG, exactly as many rebounds, assists, and steals as the average PG, a tiny bit more blocks, and pretty similar efficiency numbers. Simon Fontecchio is the most average SF, Kevin Huerter is the most average SG, Grant Williams is the most average PF, and Jonas Valanciunas (not in the screenshot) is the most average C.

Trayce Jackson-Davis of the Golden State Warriors is the least average player of the 2023-24 NBA season. Due to being a PF that plays heavy minutes at C due to the Warriors system, he averages way more rebounds and blocks per possession than the average PF, and his 3P% of 0 is way worse than the average PF. Amen Thompson is the least average SF, SGA is the least average PG (gets a lot of points and stocks), Dalen Terry is the least average SG, and Andre Drummond (who is still an incredible rebounder, and gets lots of steals) is the least average C.

Miscellaneous:

  • Luka Doncic is, as expected, the player with the highest points per possession relative to the median by their position. Jokic, Kawhi, Giannis, Jaylen Brown, SGA, Brunson, Ant, DeMar, and Cam Thomas fill out the top 10.

  • Josh Giddey has the highest rebounds per possession relative to the median, as he gets a whopping 12.3 per 100 possessions despite being an SG. Doncic, Drummond, Jabari Walker, Amen Thompson, Barnes, Westbrook, Giannis, Podz, and Josh Hart are the other 9.

  • Of course, the player with the highest assists per possession relative to the median by position is Nikola Jokic. Other names include Ingles, LeBron, Ingram, Hali, Sabonis, TJ McConnell, Kyle Anderson, Olynyk, and DeMar.
