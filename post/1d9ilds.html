Any reaction to CLIPPED (on Hulu)?

CLIPPED is a new scripted show on Hulu , centered around the Donald Sterling scandal. Laurence Fishburne stars as Doc Rivers. The first two episodes dropped through the app this week.

Of course, this isn’t an ad for it. Personally I thought the show was about as mediocre as you’d expect. It’s entertaining to watch to see how they dramatized the story, but it’s not very skillfully done.

The biggest struggle is the writing. Maybe we’re biased as hardcore basketball fans who know all these storylines already, but there’s a boatload of clumsy exposition (which serves to give information to the audience rather than the characters.) For example, when Doc Rivers meets with the Clippers’ front office, he asks them (rhetorically) how many titles they’ve won. How many times have they been out of the second round? Almost every conversation in the pilot episode is crowbarring in information to the audience rather than letting the characters have organic moments. It felt a step above one of those bad Lifetime reenactment movies (about Meghan Markle or Amanda Knox or whatever).

To be fair, I only watched the pilot episode so maybe the show settles into its own and gets better. And there are some positives. Fishburne is always a pro. Jacki Weaver (who plays Mrs. Sterling) is a top notch actress who adds some much-needed subtlety. Donald Sterling (played by Ed O’Neil) and some of the Clippers players may have been miscast though, I fear.
