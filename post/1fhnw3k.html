Is there an advanced stat that takes difficulty level of shot attempts into account?

I’m curious if there are any analytics or advanced stat that would account for the degree of difficulty involved in different attempts of the same shot type (e.g. a contested step back 3 from 26 ft out vs an open 22 ft corner 3 in transition)? I think a metric like this could help people look beyond percentages to show why certain players are more valuable despite having lower FG or 3pt percentages.

For example, in the 2014-15 regular season, Kyle Korver was the leader in 3 point percentage at 49.2%. Steph Curry, the leader in total 3PM for the season and that season’s MVP, was 3rd at 44.3%. Despite Korver being 4.9% more accurate from 3, I would argue that Curry was still more valuable that season as a 3 point shooter because he took deeper attempts, created those shots off the dribble more often than Korver, and converted more heavily contested shots later in the shot clock. Do we have a metric that takes these factors into account to determine which players are making more difficult shots?

TLDR - Which analytics take degree of difficulty into account when determining who is a better shooter?
