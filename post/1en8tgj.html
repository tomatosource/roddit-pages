BPM of each NBA champion from 2000-2024

In the latest video from thinking basketball, he made a chart showing the BPM of players from each NBA championship team. I thought it was cool and tried to do it using BPM from Basketball Reference. I included the BPM of the 5 starters from each team from 2000 to 2004 and calculated the overall average and the average for each team. There are 10 teams with at least 3 players above the overall BPM average (02, 04, 05, 08, 09, 11, 14, 15, 17, 18), and the team with the highest average is the 2017 Warriors. The only teams without a player with a negative BPM are 04, 07, 08, 11, 15, 18, 22, and 24. Nikola Jokic has the highest recorded BPM, almost off the chart.

Then, I removed the highest BPM from each team to assess which had the best and worst supporting casts. The top 3 are the 2018 Warriors, 2004 Pistons, and 2017 Warriors. The bottom 3 are the 2006 Heat, 2003 Spurs, and 2013 Heat.

In the 2012 Miami Heat, since Chris Bosh got injured and missed almost all of the semifinals and conference finals, the player who shows up in his place on the Basketball Reference site is Udonis Haslem.

BPM from Basketball Reference is a poor advanced statistic, and I did this more as a programming exercise and decided to share it here on Reddit.
