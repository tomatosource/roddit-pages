There are no existing NBA Most Valuable Players in the 2024 NBA Finals. This is something that hasn’t occurred in over 30 years and only twice since the 1950s.

The 2024 NBA Finals are the first Finals in the 21st century without a single existing MVP winner among competing teams. It’s important to note that this occurrence also does not include cases where players like James Harden and Russell Westbrook won MVPs after their appearances in previous Finals (see Additional Info for a clarification for 1977).

This unique event also stands out in NBA history, occurring only twice since the 1956 NBA Finals, during the 1990 NBA Finals between the Detroit Pistons and the Portland Trail Blazers. Every Finals in the 1960s, 1970s, 1980s and 1990s (sans 1990) feature a player who had previously won an MVP Award.

Additional Info: The 1977 NBA Finals featured Bill Walton and Julius Erving. Erving did win the MVP Award prior to the Finals, although this was for the ABA. However, both of these players won the NBA MVP post-1977 NBA Finals, in 1978 and 1981 respectively.
