Boston’s team bill is going from \(271 million next year to \)483 million in 25⁄26 with $253 million in luxury tax. For a franchise that is already in the upper echelon, and who already has many titles, could the franchise appreciate enough with additional titles for the purchase to make sense?

I can see a few ways that paying an extra $273 in just luxury taxes could make sense:

  • Lots of appreciation potential for the franchise. You are trying to “flip” a franchise and you could easily make up that money if done well.
  • You have an incredibly wealthy owner who is not trying to make a good financial investment, they want to build an incredible team. I think of someone like Balmer who is willing to spend without the return being as crucial.

I don’t see these approaches working for the Celtics. A winning franchise is built into their purchase price now. There isn’t the “flipping a franchise” potential there. How does that extra $273 make sense?

My assumption is that the Celtics are locking down those players for the next year, to run it back before the bill gets too big, but will then make some trades to lower the bill in the coming years. We would presumably have to take expiring contracts for that to work. Or is there a way that a new owner for the Celtics could cover that $272 million by winning titles? I have a hard time imagining, outside of appreciation from flipping a franchise, that a good team can return that kind of money to a new owner to make it worthwhile.
