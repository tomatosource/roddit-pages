Bronny brings such good connective feel as a passer, he’s an active defender, and a great teammate.

But at only 6-foot-2, it’s critical that he develops a reliable jumper.

Bronny shot only 19.6% on catch-and-shoot 3s at USC. Looking at other touch indicators, in high school and college combined Synergy says he made:

  • 67.2% of free throws
  • 28.1% of floaters
  • 30.2% of 2-point jumpers
  • 26.7% of dribble-jumper 3s

The shot looks good but it hasn’t gone in often enough. Becoming a reliable shooter will be the key to his NBA success.

58 Bronny James USC, Freshman

Athletic guard still finding his way as he follows in his father’s legendary footsteps.

Plusses

Good athlete with a strong frame and length, which isn’t the most surprising thing for someone who shares genes with LeBron James. High-IQ decision maker and an ambidextrous player. He isn’t a lead guard but certainly has the passing feel to make his teammates better. Picturesque shooting form on his jumper gives some hope that he’ll be able to figure it out. Disruptive defender who fights hard, plays with active hands, and displays the athleticism to log chase down blocks and high-effort highlights. Continuing to master his defense is the clear path to success at the next level.

Minuses

Poor shooter who made only 19.6 percent of 46 catch-and-shoot 3-point attempts at USC. At the high school level, he made only 33.8 percent of his catch-and-shoot 3s on 139 attempts, according to the Synergy database.

Could he improve as a shooter? Perhaps. But he hasn’t proven to have touch: In high school and college combined, Synergy data says James made only 28.1 percent of his floaters, 30.2 percent of his pull-up 2-point jumpers, and 26.7 percent of his dribble-jumper 3s. None of these numbers are positive indicators.

Lacks a great handle, limiting his ability to regularly create his own shot or get into the paint. In the half court, he didn’t log a single dunk attempt and made only one of his five attempts on self-created shots at the rim, per Synergy.

Health: suffered cardiac arrest caused by a congenital heart defect, which delayed the start of his college career.
