Nikola Jokic headlines Serbia’s 16-man extended roster for the Paris Olympics, alongside Bogdan Bogdanovic, Vasilije Micic and Nikola Jovic

Source

The reigning and three-time NBA MVP Nikola Jokic is the main star listed on Serbia’s 16-man extended roster for the upcoming Paris Olympics, alongside this season’s 6MOY candidate Bogdan Bogdanovic, former Euroleague MVP Vasilije Micic and the Heat’s starting power forward Nikola Jovic.

The last NBA player on the squad is Charlotte’s Aleksej Pokusevski.

The roster also includes a plethora of quality Euroleague players such as this season’s Final Four participants Nikola Milutinov, Filip Petrusev (Olympiacos) and Marko Guduric (Fenerbahce), former two-time All-Euroleague selection Vladimir Lucic (Bayern Munich), Vanja Marinkovic (Baskonia), Aleksa Avramovic (Partizan Belgrade), Ognjen Dobric (Virtus Bologna) and Dejan Davidovac (Crvena zvezda).

Uros Plavsic (Crvena zvezda’s new signing), Ognjen Jaramaz (CSKA Moscow’s new signing, played for Partizan Belgrade this season) and Dusan Ristic (Lenovo Tenerife) complete the list.

Apart from Nikola Kalinic (Barcelona) and Stefan Jovic (Valencia) retiring from the national team prior to these Olympics, and Marko Guduric picking up a slight injury during a Turkish league playoff game, the key Serbian players were all healthy and available for selection.

Serbia will be playing in the same group as the US, South Sudan and the winner of the Olympic Qualifying Tournament in Puerto Rico (Lithuania, Italy, Puerto Rico, Mexico, Ivory Coast or Bahrain).
