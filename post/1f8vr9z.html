2PT shots are actually significantly more efficient than 3PT shots!

Once you take into account free throws from shooting fouls.

I calculate in 2023-24 regular season, there were 72160 made 2PT FG’s (144320 PTS), 27418 made 2 shot shooting fouls (27418 PTS) and 4392 PTS from And-1’s (3.6 converted 3PT plays per game): 176130 PTS from 132347 attempts (1.33 points per attempt). Meanwhile, for 3’s I calculate 31579 FGs (94737 PTS), 1630 PTS off shooting fouls not including And-1’s and 154 And-1’s (in 1230 games: successful 4 point plays in 1 in 8 games): 96521 from 86355 attempts (1.12 PTS per attempt). There is obvious advantage with team penalty FT’s by also drawing more fouls. .137 2PT FG’s draw a shooting foul verses .008 3PT FG’s.

Might do some rebounding analysis to see whether 3PT or 2PT shots result in more offensive rebounds later, but I can tell you just 58.8% of blocks of 2 pointers result in a change of possession while 55.8% of blocked 3PTs result in a change of possession, so 3 pointers are slightly less likely to be fully penalized (whether or not there is a turnover, there is still a loss of shot clock) if the shot is blocked.

This may all change with deeper analysis. Might 2PT shots, especially from within 3 feet where most of the advantage is, result in more turnovers?

UPDATE: I am calculating 25% offensive rebounds off 2PT missed shots and 19% off 3PT missed shots, so again in favor of 2 pointers. It feels like a offensive rebound off a 3PT’er is also more likely to result in a fastbreak as the ball either bounces further from the hoop or is blocked at the last line of defense.
