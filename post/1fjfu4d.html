What are your expectations of the Celtics bench with Porzingis out potentially the first 25-30 games of the season?

KP is expected to be out til around Christmas time after surgery on his left leg. If he’s back around Christmas he’ll have missed the first 28 games. He’s looking jacked and he’s already doing shooting drills and light conditioning so his rehab is coming along well.

Celtics will be fine without him but KP changes them as a team because of his ability to score at all three levels and the stretch ability, and the rim protection on D. Big Al Horford obviously can bring most of that to the table himself—he’s shot well over 40% from 3 the last two seasons combined. He’s not quite as dynamic obviously as KP at this stage of his career, but the shot is very repeatable and if he has space he will knock it down. He’s still a very good play finisher and effective in the post, although he’s become mostly a 3 point specialist these days…and obviously he remains a very positive defensive presence.

Celtics starting five will remain excellent and league’s best level, unless Father Time catches Al…no indication surely as it stands now but can creep at any time at 38…it does take a chunk out of their depth a little, however. Kornet, Tillman, Queta will all play based on matchups. Al played 27 minutes a game last year and 30 the year before. He’s not gonna get more than ~30 and will have periodic rest. How well do you think these guys will hold up?

Celtics allowed Brissett to walk and drafted Baylor Scheierman. I like him a lot…he’s mature and very skilled offensively. He may be asked to immediately step into the rotation. Jordan Walsh had a decent G-League season, not a very good summer league, but has shown signs of refinement on both ends in his game. Lonnie Walker has an inside track on a roster spot but not guaranteed. He’s coming off the best year of his career but been pretty volatile and inconsistent throughout his career. Celtics brass like Jaden Springer who hasn’t had much opportunity or performance yet in three NBA seasons and who was added during the deadline. Maybe this is his chance.

Pritchard and Hauser are studs, two of the best backups at their positions around, but the rest of the bench has some question. Not questions that are massively existential but there are questions surrounding big and wing depth. How well do you think they hold the fort in KP’s absence?
