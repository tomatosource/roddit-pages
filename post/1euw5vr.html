The real big news of Goran Dragic’s farewell: Chris Bosh has been cleared to play and thus gets to hit the court one last time.

Whereas Wade’s knees gave way to a sudden decline and LeBron has seemingly escaped the concept of aging, the 3rd member of the Heatles, Chris Bosh, had a very abrupt end to their career despite having some very productive seasons post-Heatles, averaging 20.1⁄7.2⁄2.3 across two seasons post-LeHeat before the NBA ruled his ongoing bout with blood clots were career-ending in 2017.

Fun fact: The remainder of Chris Bosh’s contract - roughly $52 million - was paid in deferrals from that ruling up until 2022.

The 11x All-Star and 2x NBA champion tried to return to the court from 2017 onward after the Heat waived him but was unable to go out on his own terms, officially retiring in 2019. Ironically, this was the same year the team that drafted him in 2003, the Toronto Raptors, won their first and only championship.

Chris Bosh was recently medically cleared to play and is amongst the many players expected to play in Goran Dragic’s “farewell game”, a game which doubly serves as a final farewell to the oft-underappreciated third Heatle.
