Can a Team of Pure Role Players Win the NBA Championship? Let’s Build One with These Rules!

Hello r/NBA! I’m feeling a bit bored and wanted to spark a fun debate: do you think it’s possible for a team composed entirely of role players to win the NBA championship? To make things interesting, let’s set some ground rules:

  1. You cannot select players from the top 3 teams in the Eastern or Western Conference from last season, nor from teams you believe will be among the top 3 this season. For example, I think the Dallas Mavericks with Klay Thompson have a strong chance to be a top 3 team in the West, so I’m not taking players from them. This rule ensures that the top contenders still have the players they need to stay competitive.
  2. No All-Star players from last season are allowed, including those who were selected but couldn’t play.
  3. You cannot choose anyone who is a primary option on their current team.
  4. You only can pick 8 players and assume the rest of your roster will be filled out with people of equal skill of a 9th, 10th, 11th or 12th man of the roster.

Given these constraints, here’s the lineup I came up with. What do you think?

PG: Marcus Smart

SG: Alex Caruso

SF: Jalen Suggs

PF: John Collins

C: Myles Turner

Tyus Jones

Coby White

Jabari Smith Jr.

I think they might get a pretty good record and they will be hard to face in a 7 game series, but don’t think this team can close out the W against the top contending teams with all the 3 and D players, but just missing that superstar power.
