Do you think Yuki Kawamura can have an NBA career?

Yuki Kawamura has been setting the web on fire during preseason. He’s very exciting at 5’8. Tons of flair, has all the passes and eyes behind his head, excellent timing when looking to drive and as a passer, very instinctual in the PnR, just a very mature PnR handler in general. Definitely quick and very tight handle. He passes teammates open which is a higher level of skill than just being a good passer. His ability to get into the paint really opens the floor for his playmaking talent.

He’s not a liability as a finisher either…he understands angles and how to shield the ball from rim protectors and gets the ball up at the rim quickly to prevent them from being able to reject the shot, but that probably isn’t gonna be a major part of his game at the NBA level. He’s gonna make his money as a scorer with his jumper.

There of course will be questions defensively. I think he’s been OK defensively in preseason. He’s small but strong for his size. His defense wasn’t very good at the Olympics but he had a huge load on his shoulders. He moves well although not elite laterally but whether that’s enough at his size obviously is in question.

His shooting for me is the swing skill. He does have nice really nice mechanics as a shooter. His shooting numbers have been pretty average overall, but some of that is probably shot selection. That’s been a weakness of his, but in a different role in the NBA, could be a different story. He needs it to be to make it. But the stroke is nice and he can hit the pull up 3 which is an important skill for a PG today. Also comfortable with the pull up from 2.

What do you all think? Is Yuki Kawamura a future NBA guard? His future could very well be right now if he gets a contract from the Grizzlies.
