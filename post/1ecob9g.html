Who would make the Team USA basketball roster – if it went back to “amateurs only” ?

In 1992, the NBA shook up the sporting world by forming a “Dream Team” of their best pros. Historically, the Olympics had been restricted to amateurs-only (as in, you never played in a professional game before.)

I thought it may be a fun exercise to determine: who would make the Olympic team if those same rules applied?

For this, we’re restricted to Americans who have NEVER played in the pros before (either the NBA or G-League). Some other factors to consider: one-and-done freshmen flood the lottery because they have the most upside, not because they’re the best right now. This Olympic team would be in “win now” mode, likely opting for older amateurs. Also, I’m including recent draft picks. You can debate that, but historically that’s the way it used to go (for example, young Michael Jordan got drafted by the Bulls and then played in the ‘84 Olympics a month later).

Without further ado, here would be my roster, but feel free to make your own below.

######POINT GUARDS

PG Reed Sheppard, Kentucky (now HOU)

When it comes to current value, the best NCAA players are either juniors or seniors or Sheppards. Statistically, Reed Sheppard was off the charts offensively and defensively last year. Some people aren’t sure if he can run the point, but his ability to be a combo guard helps our roster flexibility here.

PG Tyler Kolek, Marquette (now NYK)

With the backup spot, we’ll err on the side of a true point guard. As a steady Eddie field general, Tyler Kolek averaged over 7.5 assists and over 39.0% shooting from three over his final two years in college. He’ll narrowly get this spot over a 4-year college star in Mark Sears (still at Alabama) who is an even better shooter, but more of a scorer than a distributor.

other candidates: Jamal Shead (Houston-TOR), Tristen Newton (UConn-IND), Mark Sears (Alabama), R.J. Davis (UNC), Caleb Love (Arizona), Dylan Harper (Rutgers)

######SHOOTING GUARDS

SG Devin Carter, Providence (now SAC)

Anthony Carter’s son broke out as a junior, averaging 19.7 points on 37.7% shooting deep. However, his primary value to the team would be his energy and activity on defense. Last season, he averaged 8.7 rebounds, 1.8 steals, and 1.0 blocks per game from the guard position, effectively making him our version of Jrue Holiday. Of course, if you wanted to be hyper-realistic in this fictitious universe, you’d note that Carter had shoulder surgery and wouldn’t be available to suit up. In that case, UConn’s Stephon Castle could fill in for that role well, having been a great glue guy on UConn’s title run this year.

SG Rob Dillingham, Kentucky (now MIN)

For a microwave scorer off the bench, it’d be a debate between Rob Dillingham and Jared McCain, both freshmen turned R1 picks. We’ll go with Dillingham; both of those two are great shooters, but Dillingham’s more able to get his own shot.

other candidates: Stephon Castle (UConn-SA), Jared McCain (Duke-PHI), Antonio Reeves (Kentucky-NO), Cam Spencer (Uconn-MEM), Kam Jones (Marquette), Tre Johnson (Texas)

######SMALL FORWARDS

SF Terrence Shannon Jr., Illinois (now MIN)

This wouldn’t be a popular pick due to some off-the-court issues, but the fifth-year senior Terrence Shannon was arguably the best American college player this past season. He has an all-around game and thrived as a scorer, averaging 23.0 points per game. His strong frame should allow him to play SG-SF-PF in international competition as well.

SF Dalton Knecht, Tennessee (now LAL)

Let’s take another shooter (and another upperclassmen) in Dalton Knecht. In his fifth year in college, he scored as well as anyone, averaging 21.7 points on 39.7% shooting from deep. Shooting would be a strong suit of this particular Team USA. Arguably it’d be a smooth transition for them as well since the NCAA and FIBA three-point lines are both shorter than the NBA’s.

SF Baylor Scheierman, Creighton (now BOS)

F it – you can never have too much shooting. We’ll grab ANOTHER older shooter in Creighton’s Baylor Scheierman. As a fifth year senior himself, he averaged 18.5 points on 38.1% shooting from three and 87.6% from the line. He’s more than a pure shooter though; an underrated rebounder, he averaged 9.0 boards per game and could help our team out on the glass if we go with a smallball lineup.

other candidates: Kevin McCullar (Kansas-NYK), Cody Williams (Colorado-UTA), Ace Bailey (Rutgers), Alex Karaban (UConn), Liam McNeeley (UConn), A.J. Dybansta (high school)

######POWER FORWARDS

PF Cooper Flagg, Duke

After all those college veterans, we’re going to take a chance on an actual freshman with Cooper Flagg. His offensive game is still developing, but he’s a highly impactful defender already (as shown in scrimmages against the actual Team USA.)

PF Ryan Dunn, Virginia (now PHX)

After Cooper Flagg, we’re doubling down on defense with Virginia’s Ryan Dunn, who had been viewed as the top defender in the NCAA. He’d be our “break glass in case of emergency” stopper. If you’re worried about his offensive upside and you’d rather take a swing on a future star instead, you could go with Rutger’s Ace Bailey or two high school kids ticketed for a top 3 pick in the future in A.J. Dybantsa and Cam Boozer.

other candidates: Day’Ron Holmes (Dayton-DEN), Collin Murray-Boyles (South Carolina), Cam Boozer (high school)

######CENTERS

C Donovan Clingan, UConn (now POR)

If you wanted to build a cohesive team, you could theoretically take the entire UConn squad from last year and just sprinkle in some extra talent around the edges. Big man Donovan Clingan was the centerpiece from that team, with comps to sturdy bigs like Isaiah Hartenstein.

C Kel’el Ware, Indiana (now MIA)

The talented Indiana big man has gotten a bad reputation for his allegedly-poor motor, so perhaps Team USA coaches wouldn’t want the hassle here. But in terms of talent, there aren’t many 7-footers with Ware’s fluidity and shot blocking potential.

PF/C Kyle Filipowski, Duke (now UTA)

Kel’el Ware has some stretch potential, but the Duke big man / Mormon p-smasher Kyle Filipowski gives us a more natural spacer to complement the big dog Donovan Clingan. He could play minutes at PF as well.

other candidates: Ryan Kalkbrenner (Creighton), Johni Broome (Auburn)
