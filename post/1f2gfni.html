The coming NBA landscape

The 90’s-mid 2000’s was a mix of skill and bruisers, with a lot of physical play. Mostly college-farmed players with a few high-quality Euro players hand-picked in. Centers and low-post play were key, with alot of team ball, dynamic sets, and a focus on defense.

Mid 2000’s to now has seen the rise of analytics, more freedom of play, and the 3-point ball being king. A lot of screens and rolls. Guards dominate, and hybrid-big men, along with the era of the “super team.” Defense has taken a step back, and the court has expanded to almost half-court.Three of top five players are now from Europe.

The guard is changing, with Steph, Lebron, and others in their twilight years. What will we see next? I see some more Euro players rising up, possibly some surprise players from Africa or other countries not usually looked at. Everyone will try to load their team with sharpshooters and foul merchants. Teams will try to model themselves after the Warriors. Defense will continue to suffer as the analytics game is fine-tuned. Double-apron teams will dominate. Luka will be the face of the league.
