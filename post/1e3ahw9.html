[OC] Comparing Isaiah Hartenstein’s per 36 numbers to Draymond Green between the ages of 22-25

###Introduction

Much has been discussed of Isaiah Hartenstein’s rise and how he learned about passing angles as Jokic’s backup. However I think he has a lot more in common with another player he reportedly studied, Draymond Green.

Although they had very different paths to the league, one being a 4 year college player and the other, an 18 year old international prospect ping-ponged throughout the league trying to find a place where he belonged, they ended up with surprisingly similar production when considering advanced metrics and team context. So I figured that analyzing this using Per 36 metrics as well as touch per game metrics would better reflect their rise as they gained minutes throughout their respective careers to see how Hartenstein and the thunder should play going forward.

Notes:

– Touch Per Game stats weren’t included for Draymond and iHart’s age 22 seasons due to the metric not being tracked until Draymond’s age 23 season and iHart being on 3 different teams during that season leading to less accurate numbers.

###Conclusions from the data:

  • By their age 25 seasons, both players can be considered low-usage, high-impact players that you can expect to give you elite defense (top 5 in their position FG% against at the rim and deflections made; along with giving you 80+ steals and blocks in a season); being a hub connecting playmaker in the high-post to find tight windows for cutters and through dribble hand-offs for shooters coming from off-ball screens.

  • Hartenstein differs from Draymond in that he is less of a versatile switchable defender (still great switchability just not elite like draymond) and moreso focused on traditional big man skills. Such as elite offensive rebounding ability, great at boxing out for others on defensive rebounds, and setting setting good screens in the pick and roll, and being one of the top rim protectors by FG% against.

  • Hartenstein has significantly improved in his ability as a passer when looking at his assist to turnover ratio despite having a significantly lower amount of touches per game and should be given more touches in the future.

###Thoughts on how Hartenstein should fit on the Thunder:

  • A lot has been discussed about if iHart should start but I think that’s the wrong thing to focus on. What should really be discussed is how the thunder should maximize Hartenstein. In my opinion, the answer of how to best maximize Hartenstein is to find a way to get him as many minutes/ touches as possible, depending on the matchup-need, if he is a starter or off the bench.

  • The method on how to maximize iHart as a starter already exists in the 2016-2017 Warriors as there is a very similar collection of talent. That being an MVP level PG who is the heart of the team (SGA/Curry) a hyper efficient shooting who can operate as a star 3 and D player (Jdub/Klay) skyscraping rail-thin scoring wing who can operate as a free safety shot blocker (Chet/KD) next to a low-usage, high-impact player who gives you elite defense and hub connecting playmaking finding the best open man if SGA gets doubled (iHart/Draymond) and a defensive wing who’s job is to make open shots and shut down the best player on the other team (Lu Dort or Caruso/Iguodala)

  • To maximize iHart when he isn’t starting in their equivalent of a death line up, they should have him operate in a mixture of his role on the knicks and a Jokic-lite role with the second unit where he touches the ball at least once every possession and gets a chance to take more of his signature 6 to 10 foot floaters, while mixing in some of their elite shot creators who can operate a 2 man game with him like how iHart did with Brunson so iHart can focus more on defense throughout the game.
