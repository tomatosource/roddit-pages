What are surprising things about your NBA city that fans should know if they come for a game?

This thread is partially in response to the absurdly negative, “Which NBA city is the least desirable to live in?” which just results in people posting stereotypes of cities they’ve never visited. Of course, I’m more sensitive to this as OKC was trashed with a lot of uninformed comments.

So, I’ll start with some surprising things about OKC. First, it’s a city with 1.5 million people and its own identity. Don’t lump it in with conservative politics of the rest of the state. Just like other cities, OKC is far more inclusive than stereotypes. Mayor Holt (Osage Nation) is Indigenous, OK-88 House Rep (Plaza District area) Mauree Turner is the first non-binary state lawmaker in the U.S. and also the only who is Muslim, and the city council includes a gay, Black middle school teacher, Black woman, a radical urbanist and leans left. Not trying to use identity markers to ignore the problems Oklahoma has that make the news, but most non-Oklahomans erase diversity in the state when they talk about it. OKC is more diverse than cities like SLC and Portland, but I’m sure those cities are more divers then they’re given credit for too.

Anyway, if you come to a game in OKC, here are 3 things you can do that you likely didn’t know about:

  1. OKC has the best manmade whitewater center in the U.S. alongside in the boathouse district. Rumor is that the rapid kayak events for the LA Olympics will be announced for OKC soon.

  2. OKC has a good restaurant scene with restaurants like Nonesuch (named America’s Best New Restaurant by Bon Appétit Magazine) and great Vietnamese options in the Asian District.

  3. OKC has a really good brewery scene. Prairie Artisan Ales is the most well known internationally and you can rent out their pickleball court in the brewery for a unique experience. I also recommend Skydance Brewery, which is Indigenous owned and often has Indigenous art events. There are lots of other good options.
