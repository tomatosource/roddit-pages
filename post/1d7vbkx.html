World Cup champions Germany announce extended 16-man roster for the Paris Olympics, Hartenstein and Kleber left out

Source

The roster includes all 12 players who were a part of the World Cup winning squad last year, which include four current NBA players: Dennis Schroder (Nets), Franz and Mo Wagner (Magic), and Daniel Theis (Clippers); as well as seven current Euroleague players: Isaac Bonga, Andreas Obst, Niels Giffey (Bayern Munich), Maodo Lo, Johannes Voigtmann (Olimpia Milano), Johannes Thiemann (Alba Berlin) and Justus Hollatz (Anadolu Efes). The remaining player of the championship winning roster is David Kramer (Granada).

Euroleague players Nick Weiler-Babb (Bayern Munich), Oscar da Silva and Louis Olinde (Alba Berlin), and Eurocup champion Leon Kratzer (Paris) complete the list.

Knicks center Isaiah Hartenstein recently expressed his desire to play for the German national team, given his German roots via his father and him living in Germany throughout his childhood, but didn’t find his name on the announced roster.

Mavericks forward Maxi Kleber was left off the German roster again, after not playing at the last World Cup and having a spat with the NT captain Dennis Schroder, because of Kleber declining the invite to play at the last EuroBasket.

Head coach Gordon Herbert has a 3-year commitment plan in place, where the players had to commit to playing at both the EuroBasket and the World Cup if they wanted to be on the Olympic roster, which he’s obviously staying faithful to and which is the main reason for Hartenstein and Kleber being left off the team.
