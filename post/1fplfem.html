Do you have concerns about shot creation for the Oklahoma City Thunder?

The Thunder on paper are a damn near spotless team. Young, deep, led by a top 5-8 player in the association, a burgeoning sidekick, a top 3 rim protector. Two of the best defensive guards/wings and another in Cason Wallace who is well on his way to becoming one as well. A fantastic C&S player in Isaiah Joe. Hartenstein brings so much to the table on both ends of the floor and so much of what the Thunder needed to shore up.

One question I have about the Thunder going into this season and into the playoffs is shot creation. The Thunder had a very efficient half court offense last year…Mark D is a great coach that runs fluid sets, they’re connected as a unit, multiple ballhandlers, multiple actions, big to big and guard to guard screening, always at least four shooters on the floor, create advantages that they make the most of. But when games slow down in the playoffs, we’ve seen the Thunder struggle a bit, which is where you do need guys who can call their own numbers. They did miss open looks in the Mavs series, which is a product of luck, but they’re a team you can gum up a bit when games slow down, in part because they don’t have that many true creators.

Josh Giddey has limitations in his game and those limitations saw him fall a bit out of favor in OKC and saw he wanting out after losing time in the playoffs. But his secondary playmaking and creation was valuable, more than people seem to realize, for the Thunder. His poor play with the rape scandal hanging over his head hurt his rep but he recovered from that to be a productive piece offensively for OKC afterward last regular season. Think the idea is that JDub can step in and fill much of that role and we did see some great flashes in the playoffs as a passer, and Caruso certainly isn’t incapable either. But I wanna ask the community whether or not you think this is a potential concern for the Thunder come playoff time.
