Is 89-90 MJ The Best Single Season Peak of Any Player ever?

Michael Jordan in 1989-1990 regular season, the first under Phil Jackson who was promoted from head coach, played all 82 games. The Bulls finish with a 55-27 record, 3rd in the Eastern Conference.

MJ for the season averaged 33.6 ppg 6.9 rpg and 6.3 apg. He averaged 2.8(!) steals per game and 0.7 blocks per game, 3 turnovers per game. He averaged 54.8% on his 2s, 37.6% on his 3s and and 84.8% on his FTs. His TS% was 60.6% and VORP was 10.6 (BPM 11.2).

In the playoffs the Bulls would go all the way to the Conference Finals, where they would matchup with the Bad Boys again. Pippen would have a migraine before game 7 and the Bulls lose in 7.

In the 16 playoff games MJ played, he averages 36.7 ppg 7.2 rpg 6.8 apg 2.8 spg and 0.9 bpg. He averages 54.0% on his 2p attempts, 32% on his 3s, and 83.6 on his FTs, for 59.2% TS on the playoffs. He had a BPM of 13.7! in the playoffs.

First round against Bucks (3-1): 36.8 ppg 8 rpg 7 apg 2.5 spg 1 bpg

Second Round Against 76ers (4-1): 43 ppg 6.6 rpg 7.4 apg 4.0(!!) spg and 1.2 bpg

And Conference Finals Loss Against The Bad Boys (3-4): 32.1 ppg 7.1 rpg 6.3 apg 2.1 spg 0.6 rpg

VORP: Value Over Replacement

TS: True Shooting

BPM/ Box Plus/Minus

MJ finished 3rd place in MVP voting this year, behind Charles Barkley and the winner Magic Johnson.

Is 88-89 season is more efficient but he had more volume this season because he ended up shooting more than 2 3 pointers per game compared to the 88-89 season, which he drained at an efficient rate. He’s had other regular seasons with higher VORP, being 87-88, 88-89, and 90-91.

So it’s clear that this 89-90 season is in the middle of the prime, but I think since Pippen hadn’t fully became great and was the reason they lost in game 7, I would say this could very well be his best season. It’s his highest scoring playoffs in the years he got passed the first round as well.
