In 1981 for the NBA’s 35th anniversary, NBA writers had a vote to determine the GOAT, best coach, best team ever, and an all-time team (greatest 11 players)

I thought this was interesting, because it’s the only example of writers voting for a GOAT during a league anniversary, and an earlier example of the GOAT debate happening.

The results were as follows:

https://www.basketball-reference.com/awards/nba_35th_anniversary.html

GOAT: Bill Russell

GOAT coach: Red Auerbach

GOAT team: 1967 76ers (won a then record 68 games with Wilt, ended a run of 8 straight Celtics championships)

All-time team (alphabetical order):

C Kareem Abdul-Jabbar

F Elgin Baylor

C Wilt Chamberlain

G Bob Cousy

F Julius Erving

G/F John Havlicek

C George Mikan

F Bob Pettit

G Oscar Robertson

C Bill Russell

G Jerry West

Some notes: NBA team rosters were only 11 players at the time, explaining the small squads. It increased to 12 the next year and stayed that way for years.

The biggest snub off the top of my head then would maybe be someone like Dolph Schayes or Rick Barry. From 35 years there’s only really one player (Mikan) who accomplished the most during the league’s first ten years, while 8 of the 11 were in the league at the same time in the early 60s
