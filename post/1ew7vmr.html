The importance of longevity for player greatness

This isn’t meant to be a goat debate but I’m sure will turn into one, especially because I am going to use Lebron and MJ as my two main examples. When discussing how great players are, 2 terms are thrown around a lot.

How high was a players “peak”

How long is a players “longevity”

For example, when discussing MJ vs Lebron, most ppl agree that MJ had the greater peak where his best was better than lebrons best, but lebron has been greater for longer(and not just longer than MJ he’s been greater for longer than anyone).

The argument that MJ is better than Lebron because of his peak is fair in a vacuum, but how long does a peak have to last to be more important than longevity.

For example, guys like KG and Hakeem had relatively short peaks but during their peaks had some of the consensus greatest seasons of all time. No one considers either of these guys better than lebron, and looking a tier below not better than tim Duncan who also had a high peak, but is generally ranked greater due to his longevity.

MJ had a peak well longer than these 2 examples, but by this logic that everyone uses clearly longevity is important when determining player greatness, so I’m curious to hear ur guys thoughts and what makes u view one player higher than another in terms of longevity and peak.
