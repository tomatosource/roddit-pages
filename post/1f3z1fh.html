LeBron’s First Game Canceled: Wet Court Ruins Historic NBA Debut

Did you know that he was supposed to play his first-ever pro game in St. John’s, NL, Canada, in an exhibition against the Toronto Raptors? However, right before game time, they had to call it off because the stadium officials couldn’t keep the court dry due to condensation accumulation. At the time, the stadium was home to the Toronto Maple Leafs’ farm team, the St. John’s Maple Leafs.

MLSE promised they’d come back for a future game but nothing could compare to seeing LeBron debut and they never kept their promise anyway. But he was there, suited up and set to go in a Canadian province with a population not much larger than Long Beach.

“LeBron James to make debut in Newfoundland

CBC Sports Posted: May 25, 2003

Basketball fans in Newfoundland will be among the first to see the professional debut of LeBron James, the 18-year-old high school basketball phenom who is expected to be the first overall in next month’s NBA draft.

On Thursday, the Cleveland Cavaliers won the NBA draft lottery and will likely use their pick to select James, a native of nearby Akron, Ohio.

Prior to the lottery, officials of the Toronto Raptors and the Cavaliers agreed to play an exhibition game at Mile One Stadium in Newfoundland’s capital, St. John’s, on October 23.”

“On Oct. 23, 2003, the Toronto Raptors and Cleveland Cavaliers descended on Newfoundland and Labrador’s capital for an exhibition matchup, one that was supposed to be the first basketball game played at a newly constructed arena — the former Mile One Centre — and James’s NBA debut.”

“Notable talent in the lineup that night, besides James, included Vince Carter, Jalen Rose and another rookie looking to make a name for himself in the league: Chris Bosh, a future NBA Hall of Famer.”
