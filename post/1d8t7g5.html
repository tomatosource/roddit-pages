Luka Doncic included in Slovenia’s Olympic Qualifying Tournament roster, scheduled to take place at the start of July

Source

Even though the Mavericks are in the NBA Finals, which could end as soon as June 14th or as late as June 23rd, Luka’s name is on the 16-man Slovenia roster for the Olympic Qualifying Tournament.

The group phase for Slovenia at the Greece OQT starts on July 2nd with a game against Croatia and ends on July 4th against New Zealand.

If Slovenia finishes top two of their three team group, they’ll match up with a group which contains Greece, the Dominican Republic and Egypt in the OQT playoffs (A1 vs B2, A2 vs B1, semi-final and final, single-game elimination). The semi-final games are scheduled for July 6th, while the final is scheduled for July 7th.

The winner of each of the four OQTs will earn a spot at the Paris Olympics.

In addition to Luka, the most notable names on this Slovenia roster are Denver’s Vlatko Cancar, former Suns and Heat guard Zoran Dragic, longtime Euroleague center Mike Tobey and former Valencia sharpshooter Klemen Prepelic.

Luka hasn’t missed a major tournament in his entire national team career, winning the EuroBasket gold in 2017 as an 18-year old alongside Goran Dragic, leading Slovenia to the 2020 Olympics at the OQT in Lithuania and the fourth place at the Olympics in Tokyo, as well as playing in the last EuroBasket and World Cup for his country.
