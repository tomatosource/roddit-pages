After spending the past seven seasons in the NBA, former lottery pick Frank Ntilikina heads back to Europe to play for Serbian club Partizan in the Euroleague, following his release from the Hornets in February

Source

After being drafted by the Knicks as the eight overall pick in 2017, Ntilikina spent four seasons in New York, then two seasons with the Mavericks and his NBA stint ended after playing only five games for the Hornets the past season.

Ntilikina led France to both the U-16 and the U-18 European championship titles, as well as being named the U-18 EuroBasket MVP, prior to his NBA career.

He’s a standard part of the French national team, with who he won the silver at the last Olympics and the bronze at the 2019 World Cup, and is set to play in his rookie Euroleague season.
