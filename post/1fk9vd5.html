The deadline for extending the 2021 rookies is about a month away. Over half of the lottery picks for the class have yet to or won’t be eligible for a rookie extension.

The 2021 NBA draft class has been an odd one.

There’s been a ton of franchise-level talent to come out of it (Scottie Barnes, Cade Cunningham, Alperen Sengun, Evan Mobley) even if some of the franchises that lucked into said talent have struggled to build around them or have less than ideal circumstances for one reason or another.

But while most of these players have received their own rookie extensions aside from Sengun, there are a surprising number of 2021 draftees who are looking at entering the 2025 free agency class as restricted free agency or are straight up not going to receive that second contract…though in one particularly notorious case, they did receive a second contract despite not being on their rookie contract at the time of signing.

Let’s go over the lottery selections (1st thru 14th) that haven’t been extended ever so briefly to see what their current circumstances are and whether they have a shot at eking out that newly inked deal before October 21st.

Jalen Green (2nd overall): Everyone’s favorite March merchant had a hot end to the season…as he has for the past 3 years of his career. For the rest of the year - and the 3rd season in a row - Green was inconsistent and with fellow 2021 draftee Sengun also looking at a large new contract, the Rockets might have to make a decision on who to build around going forward. Whether that’s a consolidation move for a star or a “we can’t afford Harden” trade in the offseason, it’s one of the many reasons Houston is amongst the more interesting teams in the league.

Jalen Suggs (5th overall): His fellow 2021 lottery selection Franz got a rookie max deal but Suggs has not been so lucky. He had a solid 3rd year where he was tenacious on D and his shot looked like it came together but how valuable that is to Orlando remains to be seen given the lack of extension. I don’t have that much to say about Jalen, he’s good at what he does and it’s kind of a surprise the Magic haven’t locked him up to a long-term deal.

Josh Giddey (6th overall): There were a surprising number of recent trades involving the 2021 draft’s selections but to keep it short, Giddey is not a part of the Bulls and it’s likely they want to see what a Kiddey-led team looks like and how he fits into a Chicago team that’s practically bursting with guards before they hand him a bag.

Jonathan Kuminga (7th overall): The Kum Bucket’s name recently came up as being someone the Warriors are less attached to than 2023 rookie Brandin Podziemski. This, coupled with him and the Warriors not seeing eye-to-eye on extensions and prior trade rumors suggests Johnathan might not spend the full season with Golden State though like Moses Moody, who that player they’d be moved for is unclear. Maybe a 3 teamer for some older roleplayers/starter-level players?

Davion Mitchell (9th overall): Mitchell was recently traded to the Raptors and while he’s likely to be part of the rotation, his role as the backup point guard is in contention with the recently drafted Jamal Shead. Needless to say, Mitchell is among the least likely of the players on this list to receive an extension when they’ve yet to fully secure their role in a crowded Raptors backcourt.

Ziaire Williams (10th overall): It speaks volumes to how disappointing Ziaire has been for Memphis that his role in the Grizzlies rotation was taken by a rookie who was, until the 2024 NBA Draft happened, the youngest player in the league (For those wondering, Raptors center Ulrich Chomche now holds that distinction) and “Haver of a very cool name” GG Jackson.

The fact that the Grizzlies traded up to get him when Trey Murphy III was picked using the selection they made only makes the choice look even worse in hindsight but Williams could very easily have a career rebound in Brooklyn…which if he does, it’ll make the Grizzlies essentially dumping him for nothing look even worse than trading up to get him in the first place.

James Bouknight (11th overall): This one feels like cheating to include but it technically counts because James Bouknight has not gotten an extension…mostly because the Hornets waived him.

And no one seems to want him or his drunk driving, lack of shooting presence in the locker room. Not even the Clippers who signed…

Joshua Primo (12th overall): Again, technically cheating but technically does count since Primo hasn’t gotten an extension. Unlike the Hornet delinquent, the serial creep Josh is on an NBA roster as of this moment, with the Clippers signing him along with domestic abuser Kevin Porter Jr.

Chris Duarte (13th overall): Remember how good Duarte was in his rookie year? Yeah, the following 2 seasons were not as fortuitous, to the point where he became an afterthought in the DeMar DeRozan sign and trade and will likely be playing out his final rookie contract year with Chicago before hitting restricted free agency. Will he have a bounceback on a Bulls team that is likely revving up the tank? Who knows!

Moses Moody (14th overall): Rounding out this list is someone who it’s hard to see them finishing on the team they were drafted by with how how much he’s been included in trade talks. Caruso is off the market but Moody and a pick is likely to be dangled in front of someone at the deadline. The question is whether someone bites on such a deal.
