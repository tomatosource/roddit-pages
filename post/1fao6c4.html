Which 5 consecutive Finals MVPs gives you worst possible starting 5?

Basically, if we were to take a range of years with 5 distinct finals MVPs among them, how bad can the resulting starting 5 get? Any of these starting 5s will probably cruise to the championship — finals MVPs tend to be very good basketball players. But which lineup will have the hardest road?

Some ideas:

1971-1975

  • PG: John Havlicek (1974 - Age 33)
  • SG: Rick Barry (1975 - Age 30)
  • SF: Willis Reed (1973 - Age 30)
  • PF: Kareem Abdul-Jabbar (1971 - Age 23)
  • C: Wilt Chamberlain (1972 - Age 35)

Big logjam at center. Nate Thurmond + Wilt didn’t work out for the Warriors — perhaps playing Willis Reed, Kareem, and Wilt together will hurt.

1977-1981

  • PG: Magic Johnson (1980 - Age 20)
  • SG: Dennis Johnson (1979 - Age 24)
  • SF: Cedric Maxwell (1981 - Age 25)
  • PF: Wes Unseld (1978 - Age 31)
  • C: Bill Walton (1977 - Age 24)

Possibly the best pick in terms of minimizing raw talent. This team only has 3 all stars: rookie Magic, Walton, and Dennis Johnson. And Walton is the only all-NBA player. Doesn’t seem like a high-scoring lineup.

1986-1990

  • PG: Isiah Thomas (1990 - Age 28)
  • SG: Joe Dumars (1989 - Age 25)
  • SF: Larry Bird (1986 - Age 29)
  • PF: Magic Johnson (1987 - Age 27)
  • C: James Worthy (1988 - Age 26)

James Worthy at center seems like a really bad idea. No rim protection here.

2006-2011

  • PG: Tony Parker (2007 - Age 24)
  • SG: Dwyane Wade (2006 - Age 24)
  • SF: Kobe Bryant (2010 - Age 31)
  • PF: Paul Pierce (2008 - Age 30)
  • C: Dirk Nowitzki (2011 - Age 32)

Once again, we are lacking in rim protection. Dirk is on the wrong side of 30 to be playing center. Dwyane Wade leads this team in blocks.
