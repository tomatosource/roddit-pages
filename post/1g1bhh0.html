Jason Terry and non-superstars playing 1200 games

Jason Terry averaged 74 games over 19 seasons as he played until he was 40 (or, more accurately, teams thought he was worth paying money to play until he was 40). That was a 1,410 game career which just edged out the longevity for players like Tim Duncan and Jason Kidd (who actually could’ve played more games if not for the strike-shortened 1999 season). Duncan and Kidd combined for 25 All-Star appearances (and dozens of other accolades). Jason Terry one 6th Man of the Year once. How rare is it for a non-superstar to be able to stay effective enough to play 1200 games (and be in the top-50 of all time)?
