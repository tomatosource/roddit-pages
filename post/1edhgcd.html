Daniel Theis, Karlo Matkovic, Yves Missi, Trey Jemison. Is the Pelicans C situation salvageable for the 2024⁄25 season?

The Pelicans clearly are going cheap on the C spot and will not be investing strongly in a C during the offseason. They’re instead bringing in multiple C’s with the expectation of filling the position by committee. Zion will play C minutes, David Griffin said as much during a summer league interview. Pelicans want to play fast and physical.

Theis is gonna be the starter going into camp. We know the skills he possesses…rebounds, active, takes charges and high IQ, can finish as a roller/dump offs/put backs. Can knock down 3’s when left open and can pass a little. Jemison, picked up yesterday off waivers from the Grizzlies, is a throwback C. Strong, chiseled, throws his weight around. Rebounds, strong post defender, will challenge around the rim.

Matkovic is a high level shot blocker, mobile, runs the floor very well, mobile in the PnR and can finish above the rim. He can shoot a little bit from the perimeter and can serve as a DHO guy. He may end up serving in a similar role to Nance as the Pelicans small ball C.

Missi is a bouncy rookie, who was very good in the PnR for Baylor. Lob threat, huge wingspan and big hands, mobile, a drop big who is a good rim protector and athletic shot blocker. Active on the boards and pursues balls in the air. Good offensive rebounder. He’s your classic athletic/long traditional C.

Pelicans seem to be comfortable not making a major outside addition to fill the C spot and these four players will surely all have an opportunity to stake their claim to playing time. They all bring different skill sets to the table, allowing for Willie Green to mix and match depending on matchups at the C position throughout the season.

Do you think these four can hold down the fort at the C position for the Pelicans? At least through the first half of the season and up to the deadline? Of course a good chance at that point the Pels will be looking to potentially make a higher leverage transaction at the C spot, but they seem to wanna see what they have now with their core, with DeJounte added. What do you all think?
