DeAndre Ayton, Buddy Hield, Eric Gordon, Kai Jones and Isaiah Mobley headline Bahamas’ 19-man extended roster for the Olympic Qualifying Tournament in Spain, Klay Thompson still not eligible to play

Source

The NBA quintet of Deandre Ayton (Blazers), Buddy Hield (76ers), Eric Gordon (Suns), Kai Jones (Clippers) and Isaiah Mobley (Cavaliers) anchor Bahamas’ 19-man extended roster for the Olympic Qualifying Tournament in Valencia, scheduled to start on July 2nd and end on July 7th.

NCAA players A.J. Storr (Kansas), V.J. Edgecombe (Baylor), Donnie Freeman (Syracuse), Lourawls Nairn Jr. (Bowling Green), Franco Miller (Florida Gulf Coast), Deyton Albury (Queens), Garvin Clarke (Akron) and Sammy Hunter (Akron) are also on the list.

Out of the other players, Rashad Davis (Challans), Travis Munnings (Pau-Lacq-Orthez), Jaruan Borrows (Andrezieux-Boutheon) and Dominick Bridgewater (Union Sportive Avignon) play in France, while Kentwan Smith (Lulea) and David Nesbitt (Boca Juniors) play in Sweden and Argentina respectively.

Klay Thompson isn’t eligible to play at the OQT, despite practicing with the squad, but that might change if the Bahamas qualify for the Olympics, depending on whether he gets an official release from USA Basketball and an approval from FIBA.

Bahamas will play Jeremy Sochan led Poland and a Markkanen-less Finland in the group stage of the Olympic Qualifying Tournament, and if they finish as the top two team in the group, face off in the knockout stage (semi-final and final, single-game elimination) against the top two of the group consisting of the hosts Spain, Angola and Lebanon.

The winner of the OQT advances to the Olympics and will play in the same group as Canada, Australia and the winner of the OQT in Piraeus (Greece, Slovenia, the Dominican Republic, Croatia, New Zealand or Egypt).
