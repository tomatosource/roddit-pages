NBA players Michael Porter Jr, Jarred Vanderbilt, and Tyler Herro came together in L.A. to support over 60 kids who lost family members in Israel on October 7th. They spent time with the children, signing autographs, taking photos, and offering encouragement.

More than 1,100 people were killed in Israel on October 7th, leaving countless children without a mom or dad … and while those kids attempt to rebuild their lives, Floyd Mayweather, Tyler Herro, and Michael Porter Jr. have stepped up in a big way.

TMZ Sports has learned the boxing legend, as well as the two young NBA stars and Lakers defensive stopper Jarred Vanderbilt, came together in Los Angeles on Monday night at the home of billionaire Israeli-American businessman, Haim Saban. Mayweather’s friend, Jona Rechnitz, was also in attendance.

We’re told 62 children, all of whom lost an immediate family member (mom, dad, brother, sister) on October 7 in Israel, attended “L.A. with Love,” put on by FIDF (Friends of Israel Defense Forces).
