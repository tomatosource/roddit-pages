Despite being considered a rim and 3’s team, the Celtics were actually the most efficient post up offense in the NBA this season while also being second in volume (8.1 per game, 1.13 PPP). Kristaps Porzingis was by far the most efficient volume post up player in the league.

Source

The Denver Nuggets led the NBA in post ups per game at 8.5, and finished third in efficiency at 1.10 (Indiana was at 1.11 on 4.0 per game). Boston was just behind them at 8.1 post ups per game at 1.13 PPP).

Of players to take at least 2 post ups per game the Celtics had players ranked:

#1 Porzingis (3.2 per game, 1.30 PPP). If you lower the criteria to 1 per game, Porzingis is still the most efficient, just ahead of Kelly Olynyk at 1.29 and Zubac at 1.27. But neither of those guys were volume post players.

#6 Tatum (2.5 per game, 1.09 PPP)

#11 Brown (2.0 per game, 1.05 PPP)

The average half court play generated 1.06 PPP this season.

Also, Jokic and Embiid essentially matched each other in volume and efficiency. Jokic was at 6.0 possessions at 1.14 PPP and Embiid at 5.8 also at 1.14 PPP.
