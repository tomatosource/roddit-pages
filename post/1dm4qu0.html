[OC] Does having the “best player” in a playoff series actually matter? Here are the results for the last 5 postseasons. (Results and TLDR at top)

When discussing upcoming playoff series, it’s common for people to go “Oh but team A has the best player!” when trying to predict who will win. But does this actually matter?

TL:DR: When the teams are actually equal or the team without the best player is stronger overall, it’s pretty much 50⁄50. The best player does win more series overall, but that’s usually because they’re on the better team to begin with. When the team with the best player is much weaker than the other team, it does not matter.

Overall results:

  • The best player won 53⁄75 series (70.6%)
  • When the best player was on the weaker team they won 13⁄25 series (52%). This number includes series where the teams were close to begin with however. It should also be noted that the 2023 Heat are an outlier here, and the 2024 Mavericks played at an elite level after roster changes at the deadline.
    • Outside of the last two years, best players on worse teams are 6⁄14 (42.8%)
    • In the last two years they’ve won 7⁄11 (63.6%)
  • When the best player was on the stronger team they won 40⁄50 (80%)

The biggest issue with this exercise is figuring out a way to judge who the best player is. MVP voting is great, but for example, in this past year only 9 players got MVP votes. All-NBA voting now would be amazing for this because it’s position-less, but in past years it wasn’t. Even then, what if a player (coughembiidcough) missed the games cut-off for award voting due to injury? Rankings lists? Well any smart person realizes that those are partially designed to create outrage. Advanced stats can be fun, but there still isn’t a one number metric that matches the eye test with 100% accuracy.

In the end, I opted to take it on a series by series basis (so, my opinion). I tried to put my biases aside the best that I can, and I think it ended up pretty accurate but that’s obviously up for debate. There are certain series where you’re really just splitting hairs though. If there was a close result, I’ll throw my reasoning in the column as well.

If a player was injured and missed most of/all of the series they don’t count (e.g Giannis the last two seasons). Same if they were seriously limited by injury in the games they did play.

I also decided to include which team had the higher Simple Rating System score in that season. This is because I wanted to see if the best player narrative could truly overcome a talent gap in other places.
