In 1959, while Jerry West was playing college ball at West Virginia, he took down a traditional powerhouse in Kentucky by scoring 33 points, playing through two black eyes. After the game, he said “I can’t explain why, but I always seem to play better when the pressure’s on.”

West would later earn the nickname “Mr. Clutch” for his late game heroics and strong playoff performances.
