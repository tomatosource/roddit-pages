Marcus Sasser’s Great-Grandfather, John Barber, holds the NCAA record for most points scored in a game, 188.

The Great-Grandfather of Marcus Sasser is one of the oldest living former NBA players at 97-years old. John Barber was drafted by the Lakers in 1956 and played five games for the 1957 St. Louis Hawks. His claim to fame although, is scoring over 100 points in three NCAA games, and an NCAA record of 188!! vs Chapman College.

This feat occurred February 16th, 1953, between Cal State LA varsity and Chapman College junior varsity. It was the result of an experiment by his head coach, who wanted to prove that any good scorer could put up high numbers if given the ball enough and tasked only to score. Barber was advised to stay near the basket, shoot the ball every time he touched it, and to expend little to no energy playing defense. Barber finished with 188 points in a 208-82 victory.
