World Cup champions Germany announce final 12-man roster for the Paris Olympics, two gold medalists from Manila left off

Source

Last year’s World Cup champions Germany have announced their final 12-man roster for the Paris Olympics, with two changes being made when compared to the World Cup winning squad. Justus Hollatz and David Kramer will be replaced by the Bayern Munich duo of Oscar da Silva and Nick Weiler-Babb.

The roster is led by the World Cup MVP and Nets point guard Dennis Schroder, the Magic duo of Franz and Moritz Wagner, and the Pelicans new signing Daniel Theis.

Every single other player on the roster is playing for a Euroleague team, which, in addition to Oscar da Silva and Nick Weiler-Babb includes, Isaac Bonga (Partizan Belgrade), Maodo Lo (Milano), Andreas Obst and Niels Giffey (Bayern Munich), while Johannes Voigtmann and Johannes Thiemann spent the last season playing for Milano and Alba Berlin respectively.

Thunder’s new signing Isaiah Hartenstein and Mavericks forward Maxi Kleber weren’t selected for the extended roster, despite being eligible.

Germany will be in the same group as France, Brazil and Japan at the Paris Olympics.
